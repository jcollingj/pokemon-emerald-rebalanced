# Duskull / Dusclops — Design Brainstorm

## Context

Ghost type. Granite Cave B2F (rare, L12-13). Evolves at L37. Bulky ghost wall with immunity to Normal and Fighting — perfect for the Dewford/Brawly segment where contact-move fighters dominate.

---

## Vanilla Stats

| Stat | Duskull | Dusclops |
|------|---------|----------|
| HP | 20 | 40 |
| Atk | 40 | 70 |
| Def | 90 | 130 |
| SpA | 30 | 60 |
| SpD | 90 | 130 |
| Spe | 25 | 25 |
| **BST** | **295** | **455** |

Duskull's HP 20 and SpA 30 are nearly useless. Dusclops has fortress-level 130/130 defenses but HP 40 makes them much weaker than they look. Both need significant HP and SpA buffs to function.

---

## Option 1: Pressure — The Patient Ghost

**Ability:** Pressure (forces 2 PP per opponent move)

**Build identity:** A pure stall wall that grinds opponents into Struggle — the only Pokemon in the roster that wins by running the opponent dry.

**Signature interaction:** Rest + Pressure — every time Dusclops uses Rest, it heals to full while forcing the opponent to spend 2 PP per move. A Pokemon with Earthquake (10 PP) runs out in 5 turns. A PP-limited sweeper becomes Struggling in ~8 turns of stalling. Ghost immunity to Normal means Struggle also barely dents Dusclops.

**Ideal moveset:** Pain Split / Will-O-Wisp / Rest / Shadow Ball

**Pros:**
- Unique PP exhaustion win condition — nobody else in the roster does this
- Very thematic for a ghost lurking in the dark, outlasting you
- Pairs with Will-O-Wisp for passive burn chip while stalling
- Works on special attackers (no Mummy activation needed)

**Cons:**
- Extremely passive — opponent can just pivot out to refresh PP
- Heavy overlap with Corsola (pivot stall) and Toxapex (Regenerator stall) in feel
- No real offensive threat — a smart opponent just switches repeatedly
- Pressure is the "too passive" archetype this project tries to avoid: it doesn't shape what moves YOU pick, just passively taxes opponents

---

## Option 2: Mummy — The Cursed Touch ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Ability:** Mummy (any Pokemon hitting Dusclops with a contact move has its ability replaced with Mummy)

**Build identity:** A punishing anti-physical wall that permanently curses the abilities of attackers who dare touch it, then burns them and nukes with Hex.

**Signature interaction:** Will-O-Wisp + Mummy + Hex — if a physical attacker hits Dusclops, they lose their ability (Mummy replaces it). If Dusclops has already burned them with Will-O-Wisp, Hex fires at **130 BP STAB**. The physical attacker: can't switch out of their ability loss, is burning for 1/8 HP per turn, AND just ate 130 BP Ghost STAB. Contact moves become devastating to attempt.

**Ideal moveset:** Will-O-Wisp / Hex / Pain Split / Shadow Sneak

**Pros:**
- Unique niche: ability stripping is a role no other designed Pokemon fills
- Synergizes perfectly with the game segment — Brawly's entire team uses contact moves (Mach Punch, Drain Punch, Megahorn, all Iron Fist punches) and Ghost is immune to Fighting
- Shapes opponent behavior: they stop using contact moves to avoid losing their ability
- Will-O-Wisp → Hex is a clean 2-step setup that feels satisfying
- Thematically perfect: a ghost that CURSES those who touch it is exactly what Duskull should feel like
- Rocky Helmet + Mummy punishes double: 1/6 recoil AND ability stripped

**Cons:**
- Doesn't threaten special attackers as strongly (Mummy only triggers on contact)
- Requires the opponent to use contact moves to activate; passive builds can avoid it
- 80 SpA isn't a sweeper — Hex is a punish, not a nuke

---

## Option 3: Bad Dreams — The Nightmare Hexer (Unusual)

**Ability:** Bad Dreams (sleeping opponents lose 1/8 max HP per turn)

**Build identity:** A surprising offensive ghost that puts opponents to sleep, then drains them with Bad Dreams + detonates with 130 BP Hex, flipping the "wall" expectation into an active sleep-trapper.

**Signature interaction:** Yawn + Bad Dreams + Hex — Yawn forces sleep on the next turn switch (unavoidable unless they switch, and if they switch, you get a free turn). Once asleep: Bad Dreams drains 1/8 HP per turn, AND Hex doubles in power to 130 BP STAB. The sleeping target is losing ~12% HP from Bad Dreams + another ~25% from Hex every turn. That's 37% per turn before defenses — walls melt.

**Ideal moveset:** Yawn / Hex / Pain Split / Shadow Ball (or Hypnosis for immediate sleep)

**Pros:**
- Genuinely surprising — nobody expects a "ghost wall" to put things to sleep and become an offensive threat
- Bad Dreams + Hex math is absurd: 130 BP STAB off boosted SpA, plus passive drain = very real damage
- Yawn creates a dilemma: switch (free turn) or stay in (sleep + drain + Hex)
- No other Pokemon in the roster uses a sleep-drain combo

**Cons:**
- Requires SpA buff to ~90+ for the offensive threat to be real (changes build identity more significantly)
- Hypnosis has 60% accuracy — bad feels when it misses
- Yawn is safer but has a 1-turn delay, meaning smart players switch out — which is still a free turn, but weakens the dream combo
- Wanders furthest from the "bulky ghost wall" design brief

---

## Recommendation: Option 2 — Mummy

> **Mummy is the strongest design** because it creates ability-learnset synergy that actually shapes play on BOTH sides — the player chooses Will-O-Wisp + Hex to punish contact moves, and the opponent is forced to stop using contact moves to avoid losing their ability. The two-step combo (burn → 130 BP Hex) is elegant and satisfying. The thematic fit is perfect: a ghost that curses those who touch it. And the game segment timing is ideal — available in Granite Cave B2F right before Brawly's gym, immune to Fighting, ready to strip abilities from every contact attacker on his team.

## The Math

### Mummy + Will-O-Wisp + Hex Combo

| Scenario | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Opponent uses contact move | Loses ability (replaced with Mummy) |
| Dusclops uses Will-O-Wisp | Opponent is burned: -1/8 HP/turn, physical Atk halved |
| Dusclops uses Hex | **130 BP STAB** (65 × 2 vs status) off 80 SpA |
| Combined per turn (burn + Hex) | ~1/8 HP passive + Hex damage = ~30-40% per turn to most targets |

### Rocky Helmet + Mummy Synergy

When opponent uses a contact move:
- Rocky Helmet: -1/6 HP damage to attacker
- Mummy activates: attacker's ability replaced
- Total punishment: HP loss + permanent ability removal

This is the most punishing "don't touch me" wall in the roster.

### Defensive Math (Dusclops)

- Physical: 80 HP / 130 Def. A burned physical attacker at 50% Atk hitting into 130 Def is dealing almost nothing.
- Special: 80 HP / 130 SpD. Special attackers also bounce off.
- Ghost immunity: immune to Normal and Fighting — the two most common physical move types in the Dewford segment.

## Ideal Items

1. **Leftovers** — Passive heal every turn. Sustain for a Pokemon that wants to stay on the field, use Will-O-Wisp, and let Mummy do work. The reliable anchor.
2. **Rocky Helmet** — When they hit to activate Mummy, they also take 1/6 recoil. Combined with burn: physical attackers are actively destroying themselves every turn. The aggressive anti-contact option.
3. **Wide Lens** — Boosts Will-O-Wisp accuracy from 85% to 93.5%. The entire build hinges on landing the burn — Wide Lens ensures it. Best choice when playing the Hex combo aggressively.

---

## Core Strategy

### The Curse Cycle
1. Switch into any Normal or Fighting attacker — Ghost is immune to both.
2. Turn 1: **Will-O-Wisp** — burn the threat. Their physical output is halved.
3. If they attack (contact): **Mummy** strips their ability. Rocky Helmet deals 1/6 damage.
4. Turn 2: **Hex** — 130 BP STAB off a burned target. This is real damage.
5. They're at 50% Atk, burning for 1/8 HP per turn, have lost their ability, and just took a 130 BP Ghost hit.
6. **Disable** if they try to use the same contact move again — now they literally cannot use their best attack.

### Pain Split Sustain
- When low on HP: Pain Split against a high-HP opponent restores Dusclops close to full while draining the opponent.
- Against walls trying to stall Dusclops out (Corsola, Nidoqueen): Pain Split equalizes HP and prevents the war of attrition from being lost.

### Ideal Moveset
```
Dusclops @ Rocky Helmet
Ability: Mummy
- Will-O-Wisp
- Hex
- Pain Split
- Shadow Ball
```

### Alternative Moveset (Control)
```
Dusclops @ Leftovers
Ability: Mummy
- Will-O-Wisp
- Disable
- Pain Split
- Curse
```
Slower but more suffocating. Disable + Curse together: they can't use their best move and are taking 1/4 HP per turn.

## Team Synergy

- **Brawly's gym coverage** — Ghost is immune to Fighting. Switch Dusclops into Mach Punch, Drain Punch, Brick Break. All contact. All activating Mummy. All suddenly doing half damage after the burn lands.
- **Physical attacker teammates** — Dusclops burns physical walls, then a teammate like Blaziken or Nidoking sweeps through burned walls that can't hit back.
- **Special attackers** — Dusclops walls physical threats; pair with a special attacker to handle the special side.
- **Stealth Rock setter (Corsola, Probopass)** — Dusclops forces switches; Stealth Rock chips every switch-in.

---

## Matchup Analysis

**Strong vs:** Normal-type attackers (immune), Fighting-type attackers (immune), physical attackers in general (burn + Mummy + 130 Def = impenetrable). Any Pokemon relying on ability-dependent contact moves (Guts users beware — Will-O-Wisp feeds Guts though, so avoid that match).

**Weak vs:** Dark-type moves (immune to Ghost, resistant to nothing on Dusclops), Ghost-type attackers (both immune to Normal, hits neutrally with Ghost), Taunt (shuts down Will-O-Wisp and Disable), special attackers with no contact moves (Mummy never activates, Dusclops relies on SpD alone).

**Key vulnerability:** Taunt completely neuters the support set. Dusclops has no recovery other than Pain Split, so Taunt + sustained pressure can wear it down. Dark types with non-contact special moves (Shadow Ball, Dark Pulse) bypass both Mummy and Ghost immunity.

## Availability

Granite Cave B2F, rare (2 slots), L12-13. Rare encounter but well worth the effort — Ghost immunity to Fighting makes it premium coverage for Brawly's gym right after capture. Evolves at L37 with vanilla evolution level preserved.
