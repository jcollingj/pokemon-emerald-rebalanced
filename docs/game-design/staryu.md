# Staryu / Starmie (#120 / #121)

## Build Identity

Starmie is a **Natural Cure sweeper** that uses its elite 115 SpA and 115 Speed to blast through opponents with unmatched coverage, then pivots out to heal status conditions on switch — ready to sweep again.

---

## Encounter

Staryu replaces Toxapex as the Route 104 surfing encounter (Gen 1–3 only). It requires Surf to access, making it a rewarding "come back later" Pokemon.

---

## Ability

**Natural Cure** (primary) — Heals status conditions (burn, poison, paralysis, sleep, freeze) when switching out.

**Why Natural Cure over Analytic or Illuminate?**

Analytic (1.3x damage when moving last) creates a contradiction: Starmie's 115 Speed means it almost never moves last. Illuminate is purely flavor with no combat utility.

Natural Cure transforms Starmie into a **hit-and-run pivot sweeper**: attack with coverage, absorb a status hit, switch out to heal it, switch back in fresh. The ability directly shapes how you play the Pokemon — always moving, always threatening, never crippled by status.

## Evolution

**Staryu → Starmie at Level 16** (changed from Water Stone)

Fits the standard 2-stage evolution schedule. Level 16 is a natural power spike where Staryu learns Recover and immediately evolves into a fully-equipped sweeper.

## Core Strategy

Starmie's game plan is simple: **enter, threaten, pivot**.

1. Switch in on a favorable matchup
2. Hit the opponent with the best coverage move (Surf, Psychic, Ice Beam, Thunderbolt, Power Gem)
3. If you get statused, switch out — Natural Cure heals it instantly
4. Rapid Spin clears entry hazards before you take chip damage

With 115 Speed, Starmie outspeeds most of the game's threats. You rarely need to take a hit. When you do (especially status moves), Natural Cure makes them irrelevant.

**Choice Specs Starmie** is the flagship set: pick your coverage move, switch constantly, never commit to a bad matchup.

## Team Synergy

**Starmie loves:**
- Teammates that appreciate hazard removal (Rapid Spin) — Charizard, Gyarados, Salamence
- Teammates that can absorb Electric and Ghost attacks Starmie fears — Swampert (Electric immunity via Ground), Gardevoir (Normal attacks don't matter)
- Physical attackers who appreciate Starmie wearing down special walls — Gallade, Nidoking

**Starmie dislikes:**
- Specially bulky Pokemon that survive multiple hits — Blissey, Snorlax
- Shadow Ball / Pursuit users that threaten the pivot (Ghost/Dark moves hit both typings)
- Electric-types that outspeed it or threaten on the switch

---

## Matchups

**Strong vs:**
- Grass, Fire, Rock, Ground types (Surf)
- Fighting, Poison (Psychic)
- Dragon, Grass, Flying (Ice Beam)
- Water, Flying (Thunderbolt)
- Fire, Ice, Flying (Power Gem)

**Weak vs:**
- Ghost-types (immune to Normal, resists Water/Psychic)
- Dark-types (immune to Psychic, can threaten with Crunch/Pursuit)
- Specially bulky Electric-types (Jolteon, Raichu — speed tie or faster, hit hard)
- Priority moves (Extremespeed, Sucker Punch) bypass the speed advantage

---

## Design Notes

Starmie is the "perfect coverage" Pokemon — the one you bring when you need to hit anything for neutral or super-effective damage. Natural Cure is the perfect ability for this role because it rewards constant movement and pivoting. The player who plays Starmie well is the player who always has a switch-in ready and never stays in on a bad matchup.

The stat buff (SpA 100 → 115) is justified by Starmie's role as a post-Surf reward. You have to work to get it (beat early routes, unlock Surf), so it should feel powerful when you do.
