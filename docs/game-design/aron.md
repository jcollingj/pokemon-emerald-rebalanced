# Aron / Lairon / Aggron (#304 / #305 / #306)

## Build Identity

Aggron is a **Def-stacking armored titan** that uses **Heavy Metal** to turn its weight into a 120 BP Steel STAB while making its 180 Def the source of its most powerful attack via Body Press. The fantasy: "I am the heaviest, most armored thing on the field. I crush you with my weight and my armor IS my weapon."

---

## Ability

| Slot | Ability | Effect |
|------|---------|--------|
| Primary | **Heavy Metal** | Doubles Aggron's weight (360kg → 720kg). Heavy Slam becomes ~120 BP against almost any opponent. Shapes the entire kit. |
| Secondary | **Sturdy** | Survive one hit at full HP. Guarantees Aggron can respond to a 4x weakness with Iron Defense or a big hit. |
| Hidden | **Rock Head** | No recoil from recoil moves. Enables Head Smash (150 BP Rock STAB, zero recoil) as an alternate offensive playstyle. |

**Why Heavy Metal over Rock Head as primary?**

Rock Head is one-dimensional: get Head Smash, use Head Smash, win. Heavy Metal creates a richer decision tree. Does the player set up Iron Defense for Body Press? Go for immediate heavy slam pressure? Save Rock Polish for a late-game sweep? The interplay between Heavy Slam, Iron Defense, and Body Press gives Aggron strategic depth that Rock Head doesn't offer.

### Learnset Notes

- **L8 Metal Claw**: The build "turns on" here — Steel STAB that Aron naturally knows. You catch it in Rusturf Tunnel at L10 and immediately have a STAB move.
- **L16 Iron Defense**: Before first evolution, Aron can start stacking Def. Sets up the Body Press payoff 20 levels later.
- **L32 Heavy Slam**: Evolution unlock. Lairon arrives with its signature weight-based move. At 720kg (Heavy Metal), this is 120 BP vs virtually every opponent.
- **L36 Body Press**: One Iron Defense raises Def to 360 (Aggron effective) — Body Press then hits for over 200 effective BP. Two Iron Defenses and this nukes anything.
- **L42 Rock Polish**: Post-evolution unlock. Aggron can go from tank to sweeper: Iron Defense first, then Rock Polish, then Heavy Slam everything.
- **L48 Head Smash**: The hidden ability Rock Head payoff. Alt build: skip Body Press, go straight to recoil-free 150 BP Rock STAB.

## Core Strategy

**Standard setup win-con:**
1. Switch into a physical attacker (180 Def walls most physical hits)
2. Use Iron Defense — Def doubles to 360
3. Heavy Slam for 120 BP Steel STAB against most opponents
4. Body Press now hits for ~270 BP equivalent off the 360 Def
5. Opponents have to pivot out or get crushed

**Sturdy pivot:**
- Take a 4x Fighting/Ground hit at full HP (Sturdy triggers)
- Respond with Iron Defense or Body Press
- Rocky Helmet chips the attacker for the "how did I survive that" moment

**Rock Polish sweep (with Rock Head):**
- Iron Defense first (+2 Def)
- Rock Polish (+2 Speed — now at 100 Spe)
- Head Smash for 150 BP zero-recoil Rock STAB
- Heavy Slam as Steel coverage

## Team Synergy

**Works well with:**
- **Paralysis support** (Gardevoir, Breloom) — patches Aggron's low Speed for free
- **Spore/Sleep setters** — free turns to set up Iron Defense without taking damage
- **Special defensive wall partners** — Aggron covers physical; it needs a special partner

**Counters to watch for:**
- **Fighting/Ground wallbreakers** — Machamp, Dugtrio still threaten unless Sturdy is intact
- **Special Fire/Water** — SpD is patched (70) but still the weak link
- **Encore** — If Encore'd into Iron Defense, Aggron is setup bait

---

## Matchups

**Strong against:**
- Normal, Flying, Rock, Bug, Dragon, Ice, Fairy (10 resistances)
- Most physical attackers (180 Def)
- Any Pokemon lighter than 720kg with Heavy Slam (which is almost everything)

**Weak against:**
- Fighting (4x) — ironic given Body Press
- Ground (4x) — Earthquake from almost anything
- Special attackers with Fire/Water/Fighting moves
