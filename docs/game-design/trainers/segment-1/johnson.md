# Johnson (Youngster) — Route 116

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Poochyena | 9 | None | Guts | Bite, Quick Attack, Howl, Scary Face |
| Taillow | 9 | None | Guts | Wing Attack, Quick Attack, Pluck, Growl |
| Skitty | 10 | None | Normalize | Fake Out, Tackle, Sing, Growl |

## Puzzle
Johnson runs a Guts duo backed by a Normalize Skitty. Both Poochyena and Taillow have Guts, meaning any status the player inflicts (burn, poison, paralysis) boosts their Attack by 50% — a trap for players who rely on status moves. Poochyena's Howl + Bite is a mini setup combo, while Taillow's 70/70/70 bulk makes it surprisingly hard to KO. Skitty with Normalize Fake Out guarantees a flinch on entry, and Sing threatens sleep. The player needs raw damage, not status tricks.

## Design Notes
- Johnson's team teaches "don't rely on status" — two Guts users punish burn/poison/paralysis
- Poochyena and Taillow from Route 116 wild encounters, Skitty also from Route 116
- Skitty's Normalize makes all moves Normal type — the player learns this means it can't hit Ghost types
- Scary Face on Poochyena halves the player's Speed, creating space for Bite flinches
- The team is all Normal or Normal-dual types, creating a subtle Fighting-type weakness the player can exploit
