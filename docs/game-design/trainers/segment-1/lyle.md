# Lyle (Bug Catcher) — Petalburg Woods

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Wurmple | 6 | None | Technician | Tackle, Poison Sting, String Shot, Pin Missile |
| Silcoon | 7 | None | Shed Skin | Harden, Tackle |
| Beautifly | 9 | None | Tinted Lens | Bug Buzz, Gust, Sleep Powder, Absorb |
| Caterpie | 7 | None | Shield Dust | Tackle, String Shot, Bug Bite |

## Puzzle
Lyle is the Beautifly-path showcase. Wurmple and Caterpie are early-stage bugs that chip and slow with String Shot, but Beautifly is the ace. Tinted Lens Bug Buzz deals at least neutral damage to everything, and Sleep Powder forces the player to either sacrifice a sleeping Pokemon or waste turns. Shield Dust on Caterpie means the player's moves can't trigger secondary effects against it — a subtle defensive mechanic lesson.

## Design Notes
- Lyle is the "Beautifly path" thematic twin to James's "Dustox path"
- The team builds toward Beautifly as the clear star — early bugs are warm-ups
- Tinted Lens + Bug Buzz at 90 BP is strong for Petalburg Woods levels
- Sleep Powder forces the player to carry Awakenings or switch, teaching item/switch management
- Caterpie is a trainer-only Pokemon (not in Petalburg Woods pool) — Lyle caught it elsewhere
- Silcoon between Wurmple and Beautifly shows the evolution chain in order
