# Mike (Hiker) — Route 116

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Makuhita | 10 | None | Iron Fist | Mach Punch, Fire Punch, Bulk Up, Fake Out |
| Geodude | 10 | None | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw, Bulldoze |
| Geodude | 11 | None | Sturdy | Rock Throw, Bulldoze, Stealth Rock, Tackle |

## Puzzle
Mike leads with Makuhita instead of Geodude, flipping the expected Hiker script. Fake Out steals turn 1, then Bulk Up boosts Attack and Defense. If the player doesn't stop the Bulk Up chain, Iron Fist Mach Punch at +1 Attack hits at 72 effective BP with priority — very hard to outrace. Fire Punch covers Grass types that resist Fighting. The Geodude pair backs it up: one with Rollout snowball, one with Stealth Rock for chip damage on switches.

## Design Notes
- Mike replaces the vanilla Pelipper (odd for a Hiker) with Makuhita as lead — thematically correct
- Leading with Makuhita instead of Geodude differentiates Mike from Clark and Devan
- Bulk Up + Mach Punch is the Makuhita endgame strategy in miniature — the player sees how setup + priority works together
- The higher-level Geodude (L11) carries Stealth Rock, the first time a Route 116 trainer uses entry hazards
- Stealth Rock on Geodude teaches the player about hazard damage on switching
- Mike is the hardest Hiker on Route 116 — L10-11 range with a setup sweeper lead
