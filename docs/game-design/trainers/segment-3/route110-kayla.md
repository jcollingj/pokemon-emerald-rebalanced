# Kayla (Cyclist F) — Route 110

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Wingull | 20 | Oran Berry | Keen Eye | Water Pulse, Air Slash, Quick Attack, Roost |
| Electrike | 21 | None | Surge Surfer | Electric Terrain, Spark, Bite, Howl |

## Puzzle

Kayla is an approachable introduction to the Route 110 Electric theme for players coming off the Slateport content. Her team covers each other's weaknesses in a clean, legible way.

Wingull leads — Water Pulse hits for confusion chip and coverage, Air Slash flinches, Roost stalls out damaged attackers, Quick Attack prevents being revenge-killed. Wingull handles Ground, Rock, and Fire types that might expect an easy matchup. When Wingull falls, Electrike sets Electric Terrain and outspeeds everything at 220 effective Speed.

The one-answer trap: lead a Ground type to wall Electrike → Wingull's Water Pulse covers it. Lead an Electric type to resist Wingull → Electrike's terrain Spark does massive damage. The player needs to sequence correctly: use a non-Electric, non-Ground type to clean up Wingull, then switch to Ground after terrain is set.

## Design Notes

- Kayla is the first trainer on Route 110 proper (not the elevated Cycling Road). She should be easier than the Triathletes — a warmup, not a gauntlet.
- Two Pokemon only, clean coverage pivot. The puzzle is "don't lead a type that's countered by her second Pokemon."
- Oran Berry on Wingull gives it a tiny healing bump so it doesn't collapse to the first hit. Combined with Roost, Wingull can stall for 2-3 turns against weak attackers.
- Electrike has no item and is fragile — it's meant to clean up, not tank. Once terrain is up, it just needs to land one or two Sparks on the weakened team.
- Howl gives Electrike a small Attack boost for Bite coverage if the player leads a Psychic or Ghost type to dodge Electric moves. At L21 this rarely matters, but it's there.
