# Jade (Swimmer F) — Route 109

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Wingull | 21 | Damp Rock | Drizzle | Water Pulse, Wing Attack, U-turn, Roost |
| Poliwag | 21 | Mystic Water | Swift Swim | Water Pulse, Hypnosis, Rain Dance, Mud Shot |

## Puzzle

Jade runs a rain team introduction. Wingull enters and automatically sets 8-turn rain via Drizzle + Damp Rock. Water moves are immediately boosted 1.5x by weather. Wingull U-turns out safely, pivoting to Poliwag while dealing chip damage. Under rain, Swift Swim doubles Poliwag's Speed — a slow little tadpole suddenly outspeeds everything at the L24 cap. Rain-boosted Water Pulse hits for 90 effective BP with STAB stacked. Hypnosis puts the player's response Pokemon to sleep, buying Poliwag free turns to keep firing. The puzzle: the player MUST deal with Wingull before rain is set, or pack an Electric-type counter, or Poliwag will run through the team before the weather expires.

## Design Notes
- Jade is the "weather introduction" trainer — teaches that Drizzle + Swift Swim is a two-multiplier combo from one switch-in
- The U-turn pivot is deliberate: Wingull doesn't just die after setting rain, it safely transfers momentum to Poliwag. Players who Thunderbolt Wingull get the lesson for free.
- Poliwag's Hypnosis is the punish for slow responders — switching to find an answer? Hypnosis puts the switch-in to sleep.
- Mud Shot on Poliwag: even after rain expires, Mud Shot lowers Speed so Poliwag's Speed "advantage" doesn't fully disappear
- Rain Dance on Poliwag is a backup: if Wingull gets KO'd before U-turning, Poliwag can set rain itself (just less efficiently)
- Counter: Electric-type attacks KO both immediately. The lesson is type coverage on routes with Water trainers.
- The rival later uses Pelipper + Swampert rain — Jade is the preview that teaches the mechanic first
