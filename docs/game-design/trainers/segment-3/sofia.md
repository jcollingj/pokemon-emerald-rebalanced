# Sofia (Triathlete F) — Route 110

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Oddish | 22 | Wide Lens | Spore Cloud | Sleep Powder, Stun Spore, Mega Drain, Absorb |
| Gulpin | 22 | Black Sludge | Gluttony | Yawn, Encore, Sludge, Poison Gas |
| Seviper | 23 | None | Infiltrator | Poison Fang, Wrap, Glare, Night Slash |

## Puzzle

Sofia runs layered status control. Oddish leads with Spore Cloud (priority Sleep Powder) — the player's lead gets put to sleep before it can act. Switch in the next Pokemon? Oddish uses priority Stun Spore to paralyze. Gulpin then Yawns whatever is awake (forces a switch or induces sleep), and Encore locks any defensive move into a loop. Seviper is the physical closer: Glare paralyzes the remaining Pokemon, Poison Fang badly poisons, and Wrap traps. The puzzle: bring Overcoat or Lum Berry. Otherwise, almost everything will be statused by the time Seviper hits.

## Design Notes
- Spore Cloud on Oddish makes Sleep Powder +1 priority — it goes before every non-priority move. At Wide Lens accuracy 82.5%, the sleep usually lands before the player can attack.
- This is the "status layering" lesson: sleep, then paralysis, then Yawn — by the time Seviper arrives, the player's team is exhausted or crippled.
- Gulpin's Encore is particularly nasty: if a Pokemon used a healing move to deal with the status, Encore locks it into repeating that move indefinitely while Gulpin uses Sludge.
- Seviper's Infiltrator allows moves to bypass Reflect/Light Screen — a subtle preview of Segment 4 trainers who use screens.
- Glare (100% paralysis) into Poison Fang (badly poisons) on Seviper stacks two status conditions — the player learns that double-status isn't always possible, but that Glare works even on Electric types.
