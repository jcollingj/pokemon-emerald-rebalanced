# Aiden (Triathlete M) — Cycling Road

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Voltorb | 20 | None | Galvanize | Thunder Wave, Spark, Rollout, Explosion |
| Voltorb | 21 | Oran Berry | Galvanize | Thunder Wave, Sonic Boom, Spark, Explosion |
| Magnemite | 22 | Eviolite | Electromorphosis | Charge, Thunderbolt, Metal Sound, Mirror Shot |

## Puzzle

Aiden is the Trick House "Boom Room" preview in miniature. Both Voltorbs threaten Galvanize Explosion — Normal-type Explosion converted to Electric-type by Galvanize. This has a critical counterintuitive twist: Ground types that are normally safe against Explosion (Ground-type Explosion? No, Normal-type Explosion hits Ground for 1× normally — but Galvanize converts it to Electric-type, and Ground types are **immune** to Electric). So a Ground type is actually perfectly safe against the Voltorb Explosions.

The problem: Magnemite is waiting behind the Voltorbs specifically to punish that Ground switch-in. Magnemite uses Metal Sound (−2 SpD) on the Ground type that just switched in, then Charges while it's tanking a hit, then fires Thunderbolt — except Steel-type moves like Mirror Shot hit Ground types neutrally. So the Ground type safe from Explosion now faces Mirror Shot accuracy drops and Thunderbolt (which doesn't hurt Ground types) but **is** in trouble from Metal Sound → Mirror Shot chip damage loops.

For non-Ground types, the Voltorbs threaten real Electric-type Explosion damage. Paralysis from Thunder Wave first makes the player slower to KO before the boom.

## Design Notes

- Voltorb #1 has no item — paralysis first, then boom threat. The player has to decide: is it using Explosion now or stalling for Thunder Wave first?
- Voltorb #2 has Oran Berry so it can eat a hit and survive to threaten a second Explosion. Sonic Boom does fixed 20 damage regardless of type, chipping away at low-health Pokemon.
- Magnemite is the real danger. It comes in after both Voltorbs are gone and farms the confusion + accuracy drops the Voltorbs set up. Charge + take a hit → Thunderbolt at 4× hits anything that isn't Ground type for huge damage.
- The "right answer" fight: Ground type for Voltorb Explosions → must immediately pivot out or be Metal Sound'd → bring a Flier or Water type for Magnemite cleanup. Two-step answer, not one.
- Previews the Trick House Room 1 "Boom Room" with Voltorb + Geodude + Gulpin — this fight teaches "check the type before walking into Explosion."
