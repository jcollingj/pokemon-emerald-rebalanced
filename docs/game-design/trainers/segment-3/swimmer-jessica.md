# Jessica (Swimmer F) — Route 109

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Tentacool | 20 | Toxic Orb | Poison Heal | Whirlpool, Acid Spray, Scald, Poison Sting |
| Corsola | 20 | Rocky Helmet | Regenerator | Scald, Stealth Rock, Recover, Ancient Power |

## Puzzle

Jessica introduces the player to compounding passive damage. Tentacool opens by trapping with Whirlpool (locks opponent in, lowers their Attack by 1) while the Toxic Orb activates Poison Heal on turn 1 — Tentacool becomes self-sustaining and untouchable by status. Acid Spray drops the opponent's SpDef by 2 stages, then Scald threatens burns. Corsola follows: sets Stealth Rock for chip damage on every switch, fishes for Scald burns on physical attackers, and Rocky Helmet punishes any contact moves. Both Pokemon heal passively (Poison Heal on Tentacool, Regenerator on Corsola). The player must deal damage fast before passive damage stacks overwhelm them.

## Design Notes
- Jessica is the "intro to status stacking" trainer — players learn that trapping + residual + burns is a self-contained win condition
- Tentacool's Acid Spray (-2 SpDef) is the key: suddenly Scald is hitting much harder than its base power implies, and the burn threat makes it even scarier
- Rocky Helmet on Corsola teaches physical attackers have a hidden cost — every hit on this "rock coral" punishes them
- Tentacool's Whirlpool + Atk drop means physical attackers are doubly punished: they can't switch out AND they hit softer
- The counter: a fast special attacker with Electric or Psychic STAB that can KO both before the passive damage adds up
- Escalation: Tentacool (trap + debuff) → Corsola (hazards + sustain) — each Pokemon adds one layer of passive pressure
