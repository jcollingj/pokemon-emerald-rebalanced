# Dylan (Triathlete M) — Cycling Road

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Magnemite | 20 | None | Electromorphosis | Charge, Mirror Shot, Thunder Wave, ThunderShock |
| Magnemite | 21 | Eviolite | Electromorphosis | Metal Sound, Thunderbolt, Mirror Shot, Charge |

## Puzzle

Dylan is the "read the ability" lesson for Electromorphosis. Both Magnemites are built around the same loop: take a hit → charge up → fire at double power. The first Magnemite opens with Charge (stacking Charge's 2× on top of the Electromorphosis charge), then Mirror Shot to invite a physical hit. If the player sends in a physical attacker and hits it, Electromorphosis triggers — next Thunderbolt deals 4× damage (Charge × Electromorphosis = 2 × 2). The second Magnemite uses Metal Sound (−2 SpD) before doing the same trick with Eviolite bulk, meaning it takes more hits before going down and fires an even more devastating Thunderbolt into a softened target.

The lesson: hitting Magnemite makes it dangerous. The correct play is to KO it in one hit before it can use the charged Electric move, or to use a Ground type — but Ground types have to eat Metal Sound + Mirror Shot dropping their accuracy before the second Magnemite falls.

## Design Notes

- Magnemite #1 has no item — it will go down in 1-2 hits if the player is aggressive. The Charge setup is punished by priority moves or a fast one-shot.
- Magnemite #2 has Eviolite so it tanks one hit comfortably. Metal Sound on the switch-in means the player's Ground-type counter now has −2 SpD before the Thunderbolt lands.
- Both use Mirror Shot on the turn the player likely counterattacks — 30% accuracy drop stacks nicely with the Eviolite bulk to mess up chip-down strategies.
- Thunder Wave spreads paralysis if the player is slow to KO. Paralyzed Pokemon now take two turns to KO what they could have KO'd in one.
- Dylan teaches the "hit it and regret it" Magnemite identity before the player faces Magneton in New Mauville.
