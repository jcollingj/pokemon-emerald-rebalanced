# Nina (Picnicker) — Trick House Room 3: The Poison Room

## Concept

**The Lesson:** You need status cures — the clock is ticking.

Every Pokemon is running a passive damage plan. Tentacool traps with Whirlpool AND activates Poison Heal on itself. Roselia priority-seeds before the player can react with Triage Leech Seed. Dustox walls with Moonlight and Light Screen while Toxic ticks up. By the time the player has beaten all three, they may have lost half their team to accumulated poison damage.

**The puzzle:** Bring Antidotes/Full Heals, carry a Steel or Poison type (Toxic-immune), or bring a Taunt user to shut down Toxic. Trying to tank through without status management is a slow death.

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Tentacool | 21 | Toxic Orb | Poison Heal | Whirlpool, Toxic, Scald, Acid Spray |
| Dustox | 21 | Black Sludge | Shield Dust | Toxic, Moonlight, Light Screen, Confusion |
| Roselia | 22 | Big Root | Triage | Leech Seed, Toxic, Giga Drain, Venoshock |

## Puzzle Breakdown

**Tentacool opens** and immediately traps with Whirlpool (the player cannot switch out). Turn 1: Whirlpool traps + lowers Attack by 1. Turn 2: Toxic — the target is now badly poisoned AND unable to escape. Toxic Orb activates Poison Heal: Tentacool heals 1/8 HP per turn while the trapped, poisoned player watches the timer accelerate. Acid Spray guarantees -2 SpDef if needed to push through a resister with Scald. Scald threatens burns on anything physical that survived Whirlpool's Attack drop.

- Tentacool at L21 has Whirlpool (L8), Acid Spray (L12), Scald (L16), Toxic (teachable TM) — all natural to its design.
- The trap forces the player to fight through it rather than switch to a safe Pokemon.

**Dustox middles** as the wall. Light Screen goes up immediately, halving special damage — the player can't easily break through with their special attackers. Toxic poisons any incoming Pokemon that survived Tentacool cleanly. Moonlight heals 50% HP, dragging out the fight. Shield Dust means no secondary effects land on Dustox — Scald can't burn it, Body Slam can't paralyze it, Poison Fang can't badly poison it. Confusion provides chip damage with a 30% confusion chance.

- Dustox is the endurance fight. If the player is already poisoned from Tentacool, this is where the timer becomes lethal.
- Shield Dust answers any "just use Scald, it'll burn eventually" plan.

**Roselia closes** with priority poison. Triage gives Leech Seed **+3 priority** — it seeds the player's incoming Pokemon before they can even attack. Now the player is simultaneously draining HP to Leech Seed AND possibly poisoned from Tentacool/Dustox. Triage Giga Drain also moves first — 75 BP Grass STAB that heals Roselia + Big Root boosts drain 30%. Toxic adds another status layer. Venoshock at 65 BP doubles to 130 (×1.5 STAB = 195 effective) on any target already poisoned.

- The priority Leech Seed is the nasty surprise — unlike other seeding, the player cannot attack before it lands.
- Against a team carrying Antidotes: Roselia re-Toxics after a heal, then Venoshocks for burst.

## Design Notes

- The three Pokemon cover three layers of passive damage: trap + direct Toxic (Tentacool), wall + accumulate (Dustox), priority seed + punish (Roselia)
- A Steel type defeats all three cleanly — immune to Toxic, resistant to Grass. This is the "correct" answer the room rewards.
- A Poison type (immune to Toxic) handles Tentacool + Roselia but still struggles with Dustox's wall
- Taunt shuts down Tentacool's Toxic and Dustox's Moonlight — another valid counter
- Dustox's Shield Dust specifically counters "just rely on secondary effects to break through" strategies
- Light Screen timing matters: Dustox sets it turn 1. If the player's strongest attackers are special, that's 5 turns of halved damage and mounting Toxic ticks
