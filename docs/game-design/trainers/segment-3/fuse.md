# Fuse (Youngster) — Trick House Room 1: The Boom Room

## Lesson
**Speed kills — OHKO them before they detonate.** All three Pokemon have Explosion or Self-Destruct. But one of them is faking it.

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Voltorb | 21 | None | Static | Explosion, Thunderbolt, Thunder Wave, Sonic Boom |
| Geodude | 21 | None | Sturdy | Self-Destruct, Rock Throw, Magnitude, Defense Curl |
| Gulpin | 21 | Sitrus Berry | Gluttony | Yawn, Encore, Sludge, Poison Gas |

## Puzzle

The gimmick: Fuse's first two Pokemon will Explode or Self-Destruct if the player doesn't kill them first. Voltorb opens with Thunder Wave to slow the player, then threatens Explosion. Geodude tries Magnitude first — if it hits 7 or higher it might OHKO — then threatens Self-Destruct. The trick is Gulpin: it looks exactly like it's about to explode (round, green, bomb-shaped). But Gulpin doesn't learn Explosion at this level. Instead it Yawns, Encores, and stalls. The player who tries to "speed-run kill the round thing before it explodes" is surprised by Yawn + Encore instead.

## Design Notes
- Voltorb's Thunder Wave is intentional: paralysis makes the player's Pokemon slower, which is dangerous when Voltorb is threatening Explosion. Slow Pokemon also get OHKO'd by Explosion more easily (they can't attack first to prevent it).
- Geodude with Sturdy: if the player tries to OHKO it, Sturdy saves it at 1 HP, guaranteeing a Self-Destruct turn. The player must either 2HKO it (impossible with Sturdy saving) or use a move that doesn't Sturdy-trigger (like a multi-hit move, or simply accepting the Self-Destruct surviving on Sturdy and then dying).
- Ghost types are immune to Explosion and Self-Destruct. Sableye completely trivializes Rooms 1 and shuts down Voltorb/Geodude. But then Gulpin Yawns it before it can attack, since Sableye can't be burned or poisoned... but it CAN be put to sleep.
- Gulpin's Gluttony triggers Sitrus Berry at 50% HP, so it heals before it looks dangerous. The fake-out: the player burns resources killing round bomb-shaped Pokemon, then the least threatening-looking one is the one that makes them scramble.
