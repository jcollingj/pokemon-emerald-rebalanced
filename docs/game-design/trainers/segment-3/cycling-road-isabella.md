# Isabella (Triathlete F) — Cycling Road

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Electrike | 22 | None | Surge Surfer | Electric Terrain, Spark, Bite, Charge |
| Plusle | 22 | Oran Berry | Technician | Nuzzle, Charge Beam, Nasty Plot, Thunderbolt |

## Puzzle

Isabella shows how Electric Terrain benefits multiple Pokemon at once. Electrike sets terrain on turn 1 → Surge Surfer doubles its Speed (Electrike to 220 effective Speed) AND terrain boosts all Electric moves by 30% for the whole field. Plusle benefits from that 30% boost on its Charge Beam and Thunderbolt too.

Plusle's Technician ability boosts moves 60 BP or under: Nuzzle (30 → 45 BP) becomes a threatening 30% paralysis chip hit, and Charge Beam (50 → 75 BP) stacks SpA boosts with 70% chance. Plusle uses Nuzzle to paralyze the player's fastest Pokemon on turn 1 or 2 — halving their Speed while terrain is active and Electrike is at 220. A paralyzed lead that would normally outspeed Plusle now moves after it.

The two-answer puzzle: Ground type counters Electrike's Electric moves but gets Charged Beam'd by Plusle (neutral coverage). Killing Electrike first is the aggressive answer — but Plusle paralyzes with priority on turn 1 and Electrike already has terrain up. The player needs to outspeed Electrike on turn 1 (before terrain, when Electrike is at base 80 Speed) or accept the terrain and race to burn down Plusle before its SpA snowballs.

## Design Notes

- Electrike has no item and is fragile (45 Def, 50 HP at this level). The window to kill it before terrain is 1 turn — aggressive turn 1 play wins this fight.
- Plusle at Speed 50 is naturally slow, which makes Nuzzle's paralysis feel like a leveling move — it drags fast Pokemon down to Plusle's speed tier. In Electric Terrain, Plusle's Electric moves hit 30% harder regardless.
- Charge Beam is Technician → 75 BP with 70% SpA +1 chance. Two Charge Beams in terrain with even one boost active puts Plusle in nuke territory for its level.
- Nasty Plot is the "oh no" button if the player isn't aggressive. After one Nasty Plot in terrain, Thunderbolt becomes devastating.
- This fight previews the Amy & Liv doubles dynamic (Plusle's slow-but-powerful identity) in a simpler singles format. Players who've seen Amy & Liv will recognize Plusle's threat and know to prioritize it.
