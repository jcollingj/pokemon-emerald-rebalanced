# Kyra (Lass) — Trick House Room 2: The Lock Room

## Concept

**The Lesson:** You need a diverse moveset — if one move gets locked out, use another.

Every Pokemon on Kyra's team locks or restricts the player's move choices. Encore traps them into repeating the last move. Taunt prevents non-attacking moves. Uproar forces 3 turns of a fixed move while preventing sleep. Together they demonstrate why one-move spam loses to a smart opponent.

**The puzzle:** The player needs at least 2-3 viable offensive moves, or a way to swap out before the lock lands. Bringing a single coverage move sweeper runs headfirst into Encore.

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Ralts | 20 | Eviolite | Trace | Encore, Calm Mind, Disarming Voice, Psybeam |
| Whismur | 20 | Oran Berry | Soundproof | Uproar, Screech, Supersonic, Encore |
| Sableye | 22 | Leftovers | Prankster | Taunt, Will-O-Wisp, Feint Attack, Foul Play |

## Puzzle Breakdown

**Ralts opens** with Encore — if the player just used their best attack last turn (say, on a previous Pokemon), Ralts Encores it on turn 1, locking them in. Trace copies the player's ability on entry AND gains +1 SpA, meaning Ralts might steal something powerful. If given a free turn, Calm Mind stacks SpA and SpD. Eviolite + buffed defenses means Ralts is surprisingly hard to one-shot without a strong super-effective hit.

- If the player has a single-attack sweeper: Encore locks them, then Ralts Calm Minds into a revenge KO on the next Pokemon.
- If the player has mixed moves: Ralts falls before it sets up, but has served its purpose — delayed the player.

**Whismur mid** uses Uproar first — this locks *Whismur itself* into 3 turns of Uproar AND prevents either side from sleeping. The player can't put it to sleep (Spore, Sleep Powder, Yawn all fail). Soundproof makes Whismur immune to Screech, Growl, Supersonic, and any sound-based status the player might use. Screech before Uproar drops the player's Defense so Uproar hits harder. Supersonic confuses an incoming Pokemon if Whismur survives.

- The joke of the room: Whismur locks itself in too. Everyone is stuck.

**Sableye closes** with the nastiest lock mechanic. Prankster Taunt shuts down all status moves before the player can act — no more sleep attempts, no more stat drops, no more setup moves. Prankster Will-O-Wisp burns the player's physical attacker before they can hit. Then Foul Play uses the player's own high-Attack stat against them. Leftovers + Recover keeps Sableye healthy through the whole exchange.

- If the player burned through Ralts and Whismur with a physical sweeper: Sableye burns it, then Foul Plays it back with its own power.

## Design Notes

- Escalation: Ralts locks the player's **move choice**, Whismur locks their **status options**, Sableye locks their **strategy** entirely with Taunt + burn
- Sableye at level 22 already has Taunt (L21), Will-O-Wisp (L12), Feint Attack (L8), and Foul Play (L27 per learnset — slightly above its natural level for this design, treat as trainer-taught)
- The three lock mechanics are distinct: Encore (repeat a move), Uproar (commit to 3 turns), Taunt (no non-attacking moves) — each teaches a different aspect of move restriction
- Soundproof on Whismur is key: prevents the "just Growl/Screech to neutralize it" response
- Trace on Ralts might copy a useful ability from the player and run with it — fun and occasionally scary
