# Kim (Tuber F) — Route 109

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Tentacool | 20 | None | Clear Body | Acid Spray, Water Pulse, Supersonic, Wrap |
| Corsola | 21 | Oran Berry | Natural Cure | Spike Cannon, Recover, Bubble Beam, Rock Blast |

## Puzzle

Kim's Tentacool uses Supersonic to confuse on turn 1, then Wrap to trap and chip while the player is confused. Acid Spray drops SpDef for Water Pulse to hurt more once confusion clears. Corsola is a defensive pivot — Spike Cannon hits 2-5 times (each bypassing defense through multi-hit math), Recover keeps it alive, and Natural Cure heals any status when it's recalled (but Kim doesn't switch). The puzzle: bring a Ground type (immune to Electric, hits both), and a Pokemon that handles confusion through Persim Berry or Mental Herb — or just attack through the confusion and get lucky.

## Design Notes
- Kim's team is about disruption and chip damage rather than raw power. This is the "annoying but not deadly" trainer that teaches players about status removal.
- Clear Body on Tentacool prevents Acid Spray from self-applying, and blocks Intimidate from applying on the player's side. This is a subtle defensive ability.
- Corsola's Natural Cure is flavor more than puzzle — Kim is not pivoting. But it introduces the ability in a low-stakes way before the player uses their own Natural Cure Pokemon.
- Wrap + Supersonic is a classic Gen 1 annoyance combo — Kim is a nostalgic throwback trainer with a modern multi-hit Corsola to finish.
