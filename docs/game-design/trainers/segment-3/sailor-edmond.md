# Edmond (Sailor) — Route 109

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Corsola | 23 | Leftovers | Regenerator | Stealth Rock, Toxic, Scald, Recover |
| Wailmer | 24 | Choice Specs | Blubber | Water Spout, Body Slam, Scald, Dive |
| Tentacool | 23 | Toxic Orb | Poison Heal | Whirlpool, Acid Spray, Scald, Poison Sting |

## Puzzle

Edmond is the hardest trainer on Route 109 — the "final boss" of the beach. His team is a full passive-damage gauntlet that tests the player's staying power. Corsola opens: Stealth Rock goes up immediately (chip damage on every switch for the rest of the fight), then Toxic poisons the player's lead. Scald fishes for burns on physical attackers. Recover keeps Corsola alive to stall. The player is now on a clock: Toxic damage escalates (1/16, 2/16, 3/16...) every turn Corsola survives. Wailmer enters as the "you must act fast" threat: Choice Specs + full HP Water Spout = 150 BP × 1.5 (Specs) = effective 225 BP at full HP. Blubber heals 1/8 HP/turn, keeping Wailmer near full health and Water Spout at peak power. Dive provides an invulnerable turn to deny prediction. Tentacool closes the trap: Whirlpool locks whatever is left, Acid Spray -2 SpDef, Scald burns physical attackers. The player takes Stealth Rock every switch, Toxic escalating, Whirlpool trapping — three passive damage sources compounding simultaneously.

## Design Notes
- Edmond is near the segment cap (L23-24). He's the hardest optional trainer the player faces in Segment 3.
- The three passive damage sources are the lesson: Stealth Rock (chip on switch), Toxic (escalating per-turn), Whirlpool trap (chip + Atk drop). These are never threatening alone, but combined they're a death clock.
- Corsola's Recover is key: it doesn't just fight for itself, it EXTENDS how long Stealth Rock is active. Every turn Corsola is alive is another turn of Toxic damage on the player's Pokemon.
- Choice Specs Wailmer is the threat that forces aggression. If the player tries to wait out Corsola with a resistant Pokemon, Wailmer's Water Spout from full HP OHKOs anything neutral.
- Wailmer's Dive is specifically to deny "wait for Water Spout to drop and clean up" — Dive buys a free turn for Blubber to heal back toward full HP.
- Tentacool with Whirlpool is the trap that ends the game: by the time Tentacool is out, the player's team has taken 2+ rounds of Stealth Rock + Toxic. Trapping them prevents the healing switch that would save them.
- Counter: fast Grass-type (4x vs Tentacool, 2x vs Wailmer's Water Spout), Aromatherapy/Heal Bell to clear Toxic + burn, or a dedicated lead that eliminates Corsola before rocks go up. Most players at L24 don't have all three. Edmond is meant to be a tough loss that teaches hazard awareness.
