# Jet (Youngster) — Trick House Room 4: The Priority Room

## Concept

**The Lesson:** You can't rely on outspeeding — they always go first somehow.

Jet runs three completely different "goes first" mechanics: physical attack priority (Mach Punch), status priority via Prankster, and raw speed supremacy at 130 base Speed. Against a player who has coasted on high-speed attackers since Route 110, this fight teaches that priority mechanics come in multiple flavors — not just +1 attack priority.

**The puzzle:** Use your OWN priority moves (Aqua Jet, Quick Attack, Sucker Punch) to fight back, bring a faster-than-Crobat threat (virtually impossible), or use a defensive approach that doesn't care about turn order. A Prankster Taunt of your own shuts down Sableye's status strategy.

## Puzzle Breakdown

**Breloom leads** with the most straightforward priority lesson: Mach Punch (+1 priority, Technician boosts it to 60 effective Fighting). At 22/135 Atk, this threatens anything that isn't a resist or physically defensive. Spore at 100% accuracy puts a Pokemon to sleep — the player may burn a wake-up on turn 1. Bullet Seed with Loaded Dice guarantees 4-5 hits (Technician: 37.5 BP each × 5 = 187 effective Grass STAB). Force Palm has 30% paralysis. Breloom is dangerous even without its priority move.

- The priority lesson here: +1 priority Mach Punch wins against any Fighting-neutral target that's not extremely bulky. Speed doesn't matter at all.
- Players who sent their fastest Pokemon in first will watch it still lose the trade on Mach Punch.

**Crobat middles** with the "speed as priority" lesson. At 130 base Speed, Crobat moves first against virtually everything in the game at this point — even "fast" Pokemon the player might have. No held item means Acrobatics hits at 110 BP (STAB = 165 effective). Infiltrator bypasses any Substitute or Light Screen the player may have set up. Poison Fang badly poisons through Substitute. Taunt at 130 Speed prevents any setup or status move the player tries before Crobat acts.

- The contrast with Breloom: Crobat doesn't need priority because it wins on raw speed. But the effect is the same — the player cannot go first.
- No item is intentional: it signals the "trick" to a perceptive player. Why is Crobat not holding anything? Because the item slot is the mechanic.

**Sableye closes** with Prankster status priority — the hardest lesson of the three. Will-O-Wisp burns the player's physical attacker before they can hit, even if that Pokemon outspeeds Sableye. Taunt prevents any non-attacking moves before the player can act. Shadow Sneak (+1 priority Ghost STAB) hits even if Sableye would be outsped. Rocky Helmet punishes contact moves (a player spamming priority Mach Punch or Quick Attack chips themself down). Foul Play uses the player's own Attack stat against them — effective against the high-Attack Pokemon the player likely sent in to revenge kill.

- The triple threat: Sableye goes first with status (Prankster Will-O-Wisp), Sableye goes first with attacks (Shadow Sneak), Sableye punishes going first against IT (Rocky Helmet contact chip + Foul Play).
- This is the room's final exam: even if the player figured out physical priority (Breloom) and speed priority (Crobat), Prankster priority is the last surprise.

## Design Notes

- Escalation: Each Pokemon introduces a harder-to-answer "goes first" mechanic:
  - Breloom = "+1 attack priority" (common, player likely knows this)
  - Crobat = "speed so high you can't outrun it" (harder to answer without Choice Scarf)
  - Sableye = "Prankster status priority" (completely bypasses speed — the final lesson)
- The room rewards: priority attackers (Aqua Jet, Mach Punch, Quick Attack), Trick Room, defensive walls that don't care about turn order, or countering Prankster with a Taunt of your own
- Sableye's Rocky Helmet is specifically for players who learned from Room 1 "just hit fast" — ramming a priority attacker into Sableye burns it with Will-O-Wisp AND deals Rocky Helmet damage
- By Trick House Room 4, the player should have a team sophisticated enough to handle this — but only if they've been paying attention to priority mechanics since Brawly's gym
