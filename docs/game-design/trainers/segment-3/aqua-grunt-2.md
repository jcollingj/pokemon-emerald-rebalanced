# Aqua Grunt 2 — Slateport Oceanic Museum

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Sharpedo | 21 | — | Strong Jaw | Crunch, Aqua Jet, Ice Fang, Scary Face |
| Tentacruel | 22 | Toxic Orb | Poison Heal | Whirlpool, Scald, Sludge Bomb, Knock Off |
| Crawdaunt | 22 | — | Sheer Force | Crunch, Aqua Jet, Brick Break, Swords Dance |
| Mightyena | 23 | — | Guts | Crunch, Play Rough, Sucker Punch, Howl |

## Puzzle

**Theme: Escalation Unlocked.** Grunt 2 uses evolved forms of Grunt 1's same Pokemon — the player immediately recognizes the lineup but faces a dramatically stronger version of every threat. The key escalation: Tentacruel now has a Toxic Orb, turning the previewed trap loop into a full engine. This is the first NPC in the game using a competitive item strategy against the player.

- **Sharpedo (lead):** Strong Jaw + Water/Dark dual STAB makes Sharpedo's biting moves hit well above their base power. Crunch (80 BP × 1.5 SJ × 1.5 STAB = 180 effective off 120 Atk at L21) threatens to OHKO glass cannons. Ice Fang (65 BP × 1.5 SJ = 97 effective) covers Grass and Dragon types that resist the Water/Dark combo. Aqua Jet priority prevents priority-spamming from safely chipping Sharpedo, and Scary Face is a speed debuff that threatens to make slower Water/Dark moves land second on now-slower targets. The player's fastest Pokemon is the answer — if they don't have one, Sharpedo can sweep before they recover.

- **Tentacruel (the engine):** Toxic Orb activates Poison Heal on turn 1, giving Tentacruel 1/8 HP recovery every turn for the rest of the fight. With 90 HP and 120 SpDef, this is the first truly "unkillable" NPC special wall the player has seen. Whirlpool traps a Pokemon and lowers its Attack — preventing the player from freely switching. Scald threatens burns, Sludge Bomb poisons for more chip, and Knock Off removes the player's item if they haven't consumed it. The player either has a Ground or Electric move to OHKO before the trap sets, or they lose a Pokemon grinding it down. This is the puzzle — "do I have the right tool, or do I sacrifice a Pokemon to drag it out?"

- **Crawdaunt (setup threat):** Sheer Force Crunch (80 BP × 1.3 SF × 1.5 STAB = 156 effective off 120 Atk) is terrifying at L22. Swords Dance at +2 doubles that to an effective 312 per Crunch hit — a clean OHKO on most things. Aqua Jet at +2 is a priority finishing sweep. Brick Break gives coverage on Dark/Steel-resistant targets and also breaks Reflect/Light Screen if the player has been running screens. At L22 without a Sitrus Berry or item, Crawdaunt is vulnerable to faster attackers — the correct answer is a faster super-effective hit (Fairy, Electric, Grass) before Swords Dance. If the player can't do that, they need to out-tank the setup.

- **Mightyena (closer):** Guts + Crunch hits hard off an evolved body — if the player uses Scald or Will-O-Wisp to burn Mightyena expecting to reduce its damage, Guts converts the burn into a 1.5x Attack multiplier. Howl before Guts activates is the ideal line — boosted Attack + Guts = monstrous Crunch. Sucker Punch is +1 priority conditional on the player attacking — it covers revenge killers that are trying to go first. Play Rough covers Fighting types that might try to exploit the Normal/Dark typing. Mightyena is the test of "did you manage resources through the first three Pokemon?" If the player is healthy, Mightyena falls cleanly. If not, Sucker Punch finishes anyone on low HP.

## Design Notes

- Tentacruel's Toxic Orb is the standout design choice — the first NPC in the game running a competitive item strategy. The player encounters the "Toxic Orb activates Poison Heal" mechanic as an enemy before they can easily acquire it for themselves.
- The team is parallel to Grunt 1 but strictly more dangerous: Sharpedo > Carvanha (faster, dual coverage fang), Tentacruel > Tentacool (tank with item), Crawdaunt > Corphish (setup sweeper), Mightyena > Poochyena (Guts + coverage). The escalation is legible.
- Three of four Pokemon threaten OHKO-or-setup win conditions — the fight is designed to be a resource drain heading into the Route 110 Rival battle. Players who sweep Grunt 1 easily should still feel pressure here.
- No Toxic Orb on Mightyena intentionally — Guts works naturally if the player triggers status trying to slow it down (burn → Guts backfire). The asymmetry keeps Mightyena from being a clone of Tentacruel.
- Crawdaunt without a held item means the player can Knock Off it freely — but that wastes a turn and gives Crawdaunt a free Swords Dance in most scenarios. The correct answer is to go for the KO immediately.
- Overall flow: Sharpedo (speed test) → Tentacruel (resource sink) → Crawdaunt (setup threat) → Mightyena (attrition finisher). The fight punishes both passive and reckless play.
