# Brooke (Swimmer F) — Route 109

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Pelipper | 21 | Damp Rock | Drizzle | Scald, U-turn, Roost, Wing Attack |
| Horsea | 20 | None | Swift Swim | Bubble Beam, Smokescreen, Water Pulse, Agility |

## Puzzle

Pelipper leads and immediately sets rain with Drizzle. Scald hits for 100% effective power in rain and threatens burns. Horsea then comes in with Swift Swim — doubled speed in rain makes it faster than everything the player has at this stage. Its Smokescreen cuts accuracy, and Bubble Beam + Water Pulse are boosted by rain. The puzzle: KO Pelipper immediately on turn 1 before it can fire a move, or accept that you're fighting in permanent rain with a faster-than-expected Horsea.

## Design Notes
- This fight previews the Rival battle on Route 110 — he also leads with a Drizzle Pelipper. Brooke teaches rain counterplay at lower stakes so the Rival fight isn't the first encounter.
- Damp Rock extends rain to 8 turns — if Pelipper survives and uses Roost before you KO it, you're fighting in rain for a very long time.
- Horsea at L20 with Swift Swim will outspeed almost anything the player has. The "aha" moment: speed-checking doesn't work against weather sweepers. You have to shut down the weather first.
- Horsea is fragile — if the player brought an Electric or Grass type, it dies fast. The lesson is weathering the setup, not necessarily overcoming a durable threat.
