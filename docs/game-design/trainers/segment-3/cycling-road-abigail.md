# Abigail (Triathlete F) — Cycling Road

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Gloom | 21 | Wide Lens | Spore Cloud | Sleep Powder, Stun Spore, Acid Spray, Mega Drain |
| Gulpin | 22 | Sitrus Berry | Gluttony | Toxic, Yawn, Encore, Sludge |

## Puzzle

Abigail is the priority status lesson. Gloom leads with priority Sleep Powder (Spore Cloud gives all powder/spore moves +1 priority) — this goes before any non-priority move, even if the player's Pokemon is faster. Wide Lens raises Sleep Powder's accuracy from 75% to 82.5%, making it land more often than not. The player's lead is asleep before it can act.

From there, Abigail's gameplan is layered attrition: Gloom uses Acid Spray (−2 SpD) on sleeping targets, then Mega Drain into the SpD drop for big numbers. If anything wakes up, priority Stun Spore (also via Spore Cloud) paralyzes it before it can move. Gulpin backs this up with Toxic to start a clock, Yawn to threaten secondary sleep on the next Pokemon in line, and Encore to lock anything that uses a setup move or status move into repeating it while Toxic ticks.

The correct response: bring a Pokemon with a Lum Berry (cures sleep on first turn), a Natural Cure/Insomnia Pokemon, or a Grass-type (immune to Sleep Powder). Alternatively, lead with something fast enough to use a Lum Berry or carry a Chesto Berry. Fire types threaten Gloom super effectively, but Fire attacks are rare at this level.

## Design Notes

- Wide Lens on Gloom is intentional — 82.5% accuracy priority sleep is a real threat. Without it, 75% feels cheap; with it, losing to it feels fair.
- Spore Cloud affects both Sleep Powder AND Stun Spore — Abigail can flex between sleep and paralysis depending on what the player leads. Grass types immune to Sleep Powder still get priority Stun Spore.
- Gulpin's Sitrus Berry fires at 50% HP via Gluttony (instead of 25%) — a small sustainability boost that keeps it alive through one or two hits while it stacks Toxic.
- Encore is the most punishing move in the set. A player who uses Lum Berry on turn 1 of sleep might use it again — and then Encore locks that same non-offensive move. Forces the player to think about their Encore exposure.
- This fight teaches the player what Oddish/Gloom does before they catch one. The "wait, it went first?" moment is the lesson.
- Deliberately difficult for a player without status cure items. Abigail is a gear check: do you carry Antidotes, Awakening, or a backup Berry?
