# Rex (Battle Girl) — Trick House Room 4: The Priority Room

## Lesson
**You can't rely on outspeeding — they always go first.** Rex's entire team uses priority moves or Prankster to act before the player no matter the speed difference.

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Breloom | 23 | Life Orb | Technician | Mach Punch, Bullet Seed, Rock Tomb, Spore |
| Crobat | 22 | None | Speed Boost | Quick Attack, Aerial Ace, Bite, Cross Poison |
| Sableye | 23 | Leftovers | Prankster | Shadow Sneak, Will-O-Wisp, Feint Attack, Recover |

## Puzzle

Rex's team never lets the player move first. Breloom opens with Spore (if the player is slow) or Mach Punch (+1 priority Fighting, Technician-boosted to 60 effective BP) if the player is fast. Mach Punch after Life Orb + Technician threatens serious damage on anything that isn't Rock or Flying. Bullet Seed multi-hits break Sturdy/Focus Sash. Rock Tomb is the anti-Flying answer and slows the target. Crobat has Speed Boost — even if the player outspeeds it on turn 1, by turn 2 it's faster. Quick Attack chips everything. Sableye uses Prankster Will-O-Wisp (effectively +1 priority status) before the player can attack, then Shadow Sneak (+1 priority Ghost) for chip, and Prankster Recover to heal before taking damage. The puzzle: you need bulk to survive priority damage, not just speed. A level 23 Breloom Mach Punch through Life Orb will OHKO frail pokes regardless of how fast they are.

## Design Notes
- Spore on Breloom is the wild card: Rex may use it on turn 1 instead of Mach Punch if the player leads with a slower Pokemon. Spore has 100% accuracy but requires Breloom to be faster. If the player leads with something slow (below Breloom's ~65 Speed), they're sleeping before they can act.
- Speed Boost on Crobat means it gets faster each turn. By turn 2, it outspeeds nearly everything in the player's party. Quick Attack is "insurance" — even if the player somehow outspeeds Crobat on turn 1, Quick Attack priority ensures first contact.
- Sableye's Prankster Recover is the reason Rex's Sableye is so dangerous: it heals before the player can KO it. To beat Sableye, the player needs either a 2HKO in one turn, or a move that KOs through Recover — which isn't possible if healing before each hit.
- Rock Tomb on Breloom is a preview of the Wattson fight's importance: Wattson uses Electric types that are typically fast. Rock Tomb's Speed drop teaches the player that slowing Electric types before Wattson is a valid strategy.
- Rex is Room 4 — the final Trick House room. Her team is the hardest, and the lesson is the most important: in Segment 4 with Wattson, the player will face fast Electric types. Speed alone won't win. Bulk and priority are required.
