# Ryan (Cyclist M) — Route 110

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Plusle | 22 | Choice Specs | Plus | Thunderbolt, Encore, Swift, Helping Hand |
| Minun | 23 | Assault Vest | Minus | Thunderbolt, Discharge, Thunder Wave, Fake Tears |

## Puzzle

Ryan's Plusle + Minun synergy is a trap: Plus and Minus only work in doubles. In singles, they're just two Electric types. Plusle uses Choice Specs — locked into Thunderbolt — with strong coverage but no flexibility. If the player switches to a Ground type, Plusle is walled completely and can only use Encore (locked from Choice) or Swift. Minun then comes in with Discharge (30% paralysis spread) and Fake Tears to shred SpDef. The puzzle: Ground types hard-wall both of them. KO Plusle fast before it Encores something into a bad move, then use Fake Tears counterplay (bring a SpD tank or just KO first).

## Design Notes
- Plus and Minus are useless in singles — Ryan thinks his doubles team is powerful but has been overconfident because he always wins in double battles with friends.
- Choice Specs Plusle locked into Thunderbolt is genuinely strong (high SpA in vanilla) against anything that isn't Ground. The threat is real against a player who hasn't prepared.
- Discharge from Minun hits for 80 BP with 30% paralysis chance — against a Ground-immune Pokemon it paralyzes the whole team if the player doesn't watch out.
- Fake Tears on Minun is a clever closer: drops SpDef by 2 stages, then Thunderbolt becomes a 2× damage nuke. The player who switches in a SpD wall gets punished.
- Ryan is also a preview: in Segment 4, some Wattson-adjacent trainers will use Electric Terrain combos. Plusle/Minun doubles synergy will matter in post-game content.
