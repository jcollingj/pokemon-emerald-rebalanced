# Benjamin (Triathlete M) — Cycling Road

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Mightyena | 22 | Flame Orb | Guts | Crunch, Play Rough, Sucker Punch, Howl |
| Electrike | 23 | Magnet | Surge Surfer | Electric Terrain, Charge, Spark, Bite |

## Puzzle

Benjamin is the Cycling Road climax trainer — the hardest fight before the rival. His two Pokemon exploit opposite damage vectors, so there's no single answer that walls both.

Mightyena leads. Flame Orb activates automatically on turn 1, triggering Guts (1.5× Attack). Howl stacks another +1 Attack on top. By turn 2, Mightyena is at +1 Attack with Guts active — Crunch at this point hits very hard, and Play Rough covers Fighting types that try to exploit the Dark weakness. Sucker Punch (+1 priority) punishes switching: the player can't safely pivot without eating a priority hit.

Electrike sweeps what survives. At L23, it just learned Electric Terrain — sets it immediately on switch-in, then moves at 220 effective Speed after that. Magnet boosts Electric moves by 20% on top of terrain's 30%, making Spark at this level hit like a truck against anything not Ground-type. Charge further doubles the next Electric move if it gets a free turn.

Single-answer problems: A Rock type walls Mightyena's Crunch but gets wrecked by Spark. A Ground type is immune to Electrike but gets hit by Mightyena's Crunch + Play Rough. A Fairy type handles Mightyena (immune to Crunch, resists Play Rough... wait, Crunch is Dark and Fairy is immune to Dark? No — Fairy resists Dark in Gen 6+, and this game likely uses Gen 6 type chart. So Fairy resists Dark but is still hit by Play Rough neutral. Electrike's Spark (Electric) hits Fairy neutrally.

The correct answer: a bulky Water type (tanks Mightyena's physical attacks with Def, resists nothing from Electrike but at L22-24 HP + Def outpaces Spark damage) plus a Ground type to end Electrike after Mightyena is down.

## Design Notes

- Flame Orb on Mightyena is an NPC using a competitive item strategy — escalating from Tentacruel's Toxic Orb in Slateport. The player should recognize the pattern.
- Sucker Punch is the pivot punisher. Player using U-Turn, Volt Switch, or switching in a check? They eat priority Crunch-power damage first.
- Mightyena's Normal/Dark typing means Ghost types are immune to Crunch but still take Play Rough (Fairy is now on Mightyena's coverage, hitting Fairy types neutrally). Ghost pivot into Mightyena is safe but doesn't threaten back.
- Electrike at L23 has had Electric Terrain since L21 — Benjamin deliberately waited for a trained L23 Electrike to lead this fight.
- Benjamin is positioned near the end of Cycling Road as the hardest challenge before the player reaches the rival on Route 110 proper. Beating him should feel like a genuine accomplishment.
