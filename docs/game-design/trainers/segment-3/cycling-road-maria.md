# Maria (Triathlete F) — Cycling Road

## Team

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Electrike | 21 | None | Surge Surfer | Electric Terrain, Spark, Bite, Howl |
| Wingull | 21 | Oran Berry | Keen Eye | Water Pulse, Air Slash, Roost, Quick Attack |

## Puzzle

Maria is the first trainer that demonstrates Electrike's Surge Surfer identity cleanly: Turn 1, Electrike sets Electric Terrain. Turn 2 onward, Electrike moves at 220 effective Speed (110 × 2) and all Electric moves deal 30% bonus damage. Most of the player's team at L20-24 gets outsped once terrain is up. Spark at +30% terrain bonus still hits respectably, while Bite covers the Psychic/Ghost types the player might have on their team.

The correct answer: kill Electrike before it gets terrain up (it has no bulk — one solid hit on turn 1 wins), or lead a Ground type (immune to all of Electrike's Electric moves). But Wingull covers the Ground type pivot: Water Pulse handles Ground and Rock types, Air Slash handles Fighting types, and Roost lets Wingull stall while Electrike is reviving the terrain pressure on the next switch. Quick Attack prevents Wingull from being revenge-killed by a fast priority sweeper.

## Design Notes

- Electrike has no item intentionally — it should fall in 1 hit to anything decent. The threat is the terrain, not Electrike's bulk.
- Howl on Electrike is the "got a free turn" option. If the player doesn't KO it on turn 1, Howl + Spark starts accumulating damage in terrain.
- Wingull's Roost is the twist — the player who powers through Electrike with a Ground type finds Wingull Roosting away Water Pulses and stalling. Use Air Slash + flinch to frustrate slow pivot attempts.
- This fight teaches: "Electric Terrain changes the Speed math. Kill the setter fast, or bring Ground type — but cover that with a Flying/Water pivot."
- The team is intentionally simple (2 Pokemon, clean cover-the-weakness composition). Maria is mid-Cycling Road, not a climax fight.
