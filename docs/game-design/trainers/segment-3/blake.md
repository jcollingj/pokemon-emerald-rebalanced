# Blake (Triathlete M) — Route 110

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Electrike | 22 | None | Surge Surfer | Electric Terrain, Thunderbolt, Bite, Spark |
| Electrike | 23 | Terrain Extender | Surge Surfer | Electric Terrain, Thunderbolt, Bite, Thunder Wave |

## Puzzle

Blake runs two Electrikes. The first sets Electric Terrain (Surge Surfer). When it goes down, the second Electrike enters into terrain that's ALREADY active — instant 220 effective speed with Terrain Extender adding 3 more turns. The player faces a Pokemon that moves first, hits first, and threatens Thunder Wave to permanently cripple their fastest answer. The puzzle: KO the first Electrike before it can set terrain, or have a Ground type to hard-wall Electric moves entirely.

## Design Notes
- Two Electrikes of the same species is unusual and memorable — Blake is clearly obsessed with his "Electric Terrain combo."
- The first Electrike may die fast (low bulk), but it almost always gets to set terrain on turn 1. The second one arriving into active terrain is the true threat.
- Terrain Extender on the second Electrike extends the terrain from 5 to 8 turns. If the player can't KO it in 8 turns, the terrain resets for 5 more when the second Electrike uses its own Electric Terrain.
- Thunder Wave on the second Electrike: if it lands, the paralyzed Pokemon moves at 25% speed — but at 220 effective speed in terrain, Electrike still moves first even against a paralyzed foe.
- Blake is the "Wattson preview" trainer — he's showing the player what Electric Terrain does before Wattson takes it to a higher level.
