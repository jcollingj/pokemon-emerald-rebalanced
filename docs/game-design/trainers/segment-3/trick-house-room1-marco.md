# Marco (Camper) — Trick House Room 1: The Boom Room

## Concept

**The Lesson:** Speed kills — KO them before they detonate.

All three Pokemon are packing Selfdestruct. Marco leads with a Thunder Wave to slow the player down, then threatens instant KO-and-death if they can't close fast enough. The twist: Voltorb's Galvanize converts its Normal-type Self-Destruct into **Electric-type**, so Ground types that normally absorb Selfdestruct are NOT safe. Only Ghost types can absorb all three detonations.

**The puzzle:** The player needs to hit hard and fast. A Ghost-type blanks all three. A tanky Normal-resist can trade. But a slow or bulky team risks eating a bomb on the way out.

## Puzzle Breakdown

**Voltorb leads** and immediately threatens Self-Destruct — but the player may not expect it yet. Thunder Wave first slows the player's team. Screech drops Defense, making Self-Destruct even more lethal. Galvanize means Ground types absorb Rollout and Earthquake, but **cannot absorb Galvanize Self-Destruct** — it's Electric-type. Only Ghost is fully safe.

**Geodude middle** sets Stealth Rock on turn 1 if not immediately threatened. Defense Curl + Rollout preys on any team that decides to "stall it out" — snowballing damage while Stamina tanks hits. When Geodude drops below a threshold, Self-Destruct. Normal-type, so Ground types DO safely absorb this one, creating a mini-puzzle: the same Ground type that eats Geodude's boom runs into Voltorb's Electric boom.

**Gulpin closes** with the most dangerous detonation. Yawn first — if the player's Pokemon falls asleep, they can't KO Gulpin before it Self-Destructs next turn. Encore locks the player into a non-attacking move if they made a mistake. Sitrus Berry + Gluttony activates at 50% HP, keeping Gulpin alive for one more turn. Self-Destruct on the way out.

## Design Notes

- The room's core tension: use a Ghost type to absorb all three, or burn through them with fast attackers — but NOT slow tanky Pokemon that will eat a bomb on the way out
- Voltorb's Galvanize twist recontextualizes the "bring a Ground type" instinct — the player learns that Selfdestruct isn't always Normal-type
- Geodude's Stealth Rock punishes repeated attempts to re-enter with priority sweepers
- Gulpin's Yawn + Encore is the sneaky combo — the sleep threat is what makes this scarier than "just use Mach Punch three times"
- No items on Voltorb/Geodude (they're meant to die) vs. Sitrus on Gulpin (extends the threat window)
