# Takao (Black Belt) — Dewford Gym

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Makuhita | 16 | Eviolite | Iron Fist | Mach Punch, Fire Punch, Bulk Up, Tackle |
| Tyrogue | 17 | Oran Berry | Guts | Fake Out, Low Kick, Force Palm, Leer |
| Machop | 17 | None | No Guard | Karate Chop, Low Kick, Seismic Toss, Focus Energy |

## Puzzle

Takao is the gym's intro fight — a "read the labels" test. Makuhita leads with Eviolite + Bulk Up, becoming surprisingly hard to KO (80 HP + Eviolite + a Bulk Up turn). Mach Punch and Fire Punch hit harder than their base power implies thanks to Iron Fist. The player has to decide: attack through the bulk, or KO before Bulk Up lands? Tyrogue's Fake Out chips in and pivots to Force Palm for early paralysis pressure. Machop closes with No Guard — every Low Kick and Seismic Toss is perfectly accurate, and Focus Energy means Karate Chop crits frequently. Takao teaches three distinct mechanics in one fight, but none is threatening enough alone to overwhelm a cautious player.

## Design Notes
- Makuhita is the lesson about Eviolite — the player should expect a pushover and get an unexpected tank
- Tyrogue's Leer sets up Fake Out into Force Palm: full accuracy paralysis on Force Palm = 30% proc
- Machop with Focus Energy + No Guard is a preview of the confusion-lock mechanic Brawly's Machop will show with Dynamic Punch
- No held item on Machop intentionally — it dies fastest, teaching No Guard threat without letting it linger
- Escalation: Makuhita (setup threat) → Tyrogue (status chip) → Machop (accuracy cheating) — each Pokemon introduces one gym concept
