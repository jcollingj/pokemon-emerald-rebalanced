# Brenden (Sailor) — Dewford Gym

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Tyrogue | 17 | None | Guts | Fake Out, Low Kick, Force Palm, Leer |
| Hitmonchan | 18 | Sitrus Berry | Iron Fist | Mach Punch, Thunder Punch, Fire Punch, Ice Punch |
| Makuhita | 18 | Eviolite | Iron Fist | Mach Punch, Drain Punch, Bulk Up, Fire Punch |

## Puzzle

Brenden is the gym's elemental coverage gauntlet — a sailor who fights like he's prepared for anything. Tyrogue opens with Fake Out chip and Leer to drop Defense, softening the lead for Hitmonchan. Hitmonchan is the centerpiece: four punches, four types, four coverage angles — Mach Punch for priority, Thunder Punch for Water/Flying answers, Fire Punch for Grass/Bug/Steel, Ice Punch for Ground/Flying/Grass. Iron Fist boosts all of them to 90 effective BP. The player cannot simply switch in a resistant type and feel safe — Hitmonchan always has an answer. Makuhita closes with Eviolite bulk: Bulk Up → Mach Punch + Drain Punch sustain loop is nearly unkillable if left unchecked.

## Design Notes
- Hitmonchan is a preview of Brawly's own Hitmonchan — same Iron Fist + elemental punch kit, one level lower
- Thunder Punch is the priority threat vs Water-types (Tentacruel) that might try to stall; Fire Punch handles Grass answers
- Tyrogue's Leer is important: -1 Def on the lead means Hitmonchan's punches hit even harder when it comes in
- Makuhita in slot 3 is a Bulk Up ambush — if Hitmonchan has already chipped the player's team, Makuhita's Eviolite + one Bulk Up can be nearly impossible to KO
- Brenden as a Sailor class implies he's traveled widely and fought diverse opponents — thematically, his Hitmonchan reflects that range
- Sitrus Berry on Hitmonchan keeps it in range to throw a second round of punches if the player tries to chip it down slowly
