# Udouglas (Swimmer M) — Route 106

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Wingull | 16 | None | Drizzle | Wing Attack, Water Pulse, Supersonic, Growl |
| Tentacool | 17 | Oran Berry | Poison Heal | Whirlpool, Scald, Acid Spray, Poison Sting |
| Wailmer | 18 | Sitrus Berry | Blubber | Water Pulse, Aqua Ring, Body Slam |

## Puzzle
Douglas layers three compounding threats: confusion, a trap, and a healing tank. Wingull leads and immediately sets Drizzle rain, then fires rain-boosted Water Pulse (60 BP → 90 effective with rain) which carries a 20% confusion chance. Supersonic stacks more confusion. A confused player walking into Tentacool's Whirlpool is brutal — the trap + Acid Spray's guaranteed -2 SpDef means any remaining special attackers are getting hit much harder. Wailmer closes out with Blubber passive healing + Sitrus Berry, requiring consistent pressure to chip down. The counter is to bring an Electric or Grass type to remove Wingull fast before Drizzle enables the rest of the plan, or lead with a Lum Berry user to shake confusion.

## Design Notes
- Wingull's Drizzle is the key — without rain the team loses its water damage amplification and Hurricane accuracy bonus (not yet in Wingull's kit, but Drizzle is still active)
- Tentacool's Scald (80 BP, 30% burn) is particularly threatening with rain: rain doesn't boost Scald's Fire-adjacent burn proc but the base 80 BP is solid at L17
- Wailmer's Blubber (Water Veil rework: heals 1/8 HP/turn) stacks with Sitrus Berry — it will survive one hard hit then recover significantly
- This is the first trainer where rain matters as a mechanic — foreshadows Pelipper rain teams later in the game
- Wingull at L16 still does real damage with Wing Attack (60 BP Flying STAB) — don't assume it's only setup
