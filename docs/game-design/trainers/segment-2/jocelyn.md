# Jocelyn (Battle Girl) — Dewford Gym

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Tyrogue | 16 | None | Guts | Fake Out, Low Kick, Force Palm, Leer |
| Meditite | 17 | Eviolite | Pure Power | Fake Out, Confusion, Drain Punch, Meditate |
| Makuhita | 17 | Sitrus Berry | Iron Fist | Mach Punch, Fire Punch, Drain Punch, Bulk Up |

## Puzzle

Jocelyn teaches Pure Power respect. Her Meditite looks fragile — 50 HP, 55 Attack on paper — but Pure Power doubles the effective attack modifier. Drain Punch off doubled power heals back a shocking amount. The player who expects to one-shot a "weak" Meditite with a resisted move is in for a surprise: Eviolite means Meditite tanks the hit, Drain Punch hits back for more than expected, and suddenly Meditite is at near-full HP again. Tyrogue opens with Fake Out flinch to deny any setup, and Force Palm's 30% paralysis forces switches. Makuhita closes: Sitrus Berry activates after one strong hit, then Drain Punch + Iron Fist sustains while Bulk Up snowballs.

## Design Notes
- Meditite's Meditate + Pure Power is a bait — if the player lets it use Meditate, the next Drain Punch heals a terrifying amount
- Detect is intentionally left off here (given to harder trainers later) — Jocelyn's Meditite is exploitable by switching in a special attacker, giving the player a fair out
- Tyrogue's Fake Out → Force Palm is the same combo that appears across multiple gym trainers: chip + paralysis pressure
- Makuhita's Sitrus Berry makes it the "closer" — it absorbs one big hit, heals, then Bulk Up sets the stage for a sweep attempt
- Contrast with Takao: Takao's Makuhita sets up early; Jocelyn's Makuhita waits to counter-attack
