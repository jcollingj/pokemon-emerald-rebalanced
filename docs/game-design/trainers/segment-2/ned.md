# Uned (Fisherman) — Route 106

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Clamperl | 16 | Oran Berry | Shell Armor | Whirlpool, Water Gun, Iron Defense, Water Pulse |
| Staryu | 18 | None | Illuminate | Psybeam, Power Gem, Rapid Spin, Recover |
| Wailmer | 19 | Sitrus Berry | Blubber | Water Pulse, Aqua Ring, Body Slam, Surf |

## Puzzle
Ned is the "rare catch" fisherman whose team creates a two-stage defensive puzzle. Clamperl leads with Whirlpool (traps) then stacks Iron Defense — Shell Armor means no critical hit breakthrough, and Iron Defense means physical moves barely dent it. The instinct is to switch to a special attacker to bypass the Defense boost. But Staryu is waiting for exactly that: Illuminate boosts Psybeam (65 BP → 84.5 effective) and Power Gem (80 BP → 104 effective), and these two moves between them cover Poison, Ghost, Fighting (Psybeam) and Ice, Fire, Flying, Bug (Power Gem) — special attackers walking in to handle Clamperl likely take a super-effective beam. Recover makes Staryu durable. Wailmer closes out with Blubber + Aqua Ring + Sitrus Berry sustain, threatening the player's last Pokemon with Body Slam's paralysis chance (30%). The solution: either use a physical attacker that can 2HKO Clamperl before a second Iron Defense, accepting the physical damage, then switch to a special attacker that resists Staryu's beams — or bring a specially defensive Pokemon that can tank Psybeam and KO Staryu before it Recovers.

## Design Notes
- Shell Armor + Iron Defense is Clamperl's gimmick: completely crit-immune and increasingly physically impenetrable. The counter is always special moves.
- Clamperl has no design doc; Shell Armor (vanilla) is used. Whirlpool at L16 and Iron Defense at L18 fit its vanilla learnset pacing.
- Staryu's two Illuminate beams (Psybeam + Power Gem) cover completely different types — very few Pokemon in the player's pool at this point resist both simultaneously
- Rapid Spin on Staryu is utility — if the player set Stealth Rock or entry hazards, Ned clears them. Punishes hazard-heavy leads.
- Wailmer at L19 is nearly at Brawly's level cap — Ned is the hardest optional trainer on Route 106
- Ned's Wailmer previews the Water Spout Cannon build: Blubber + Aqua Ring sustain is already active at this level, foreshadowing what Wailord will do endgame
- Each trainer on Route 106 escalates the pool complexity: Douglas (rain), Kyla (beam preview), Elliot (drain + ambush), Ned (two-stage defensive puzzle)
