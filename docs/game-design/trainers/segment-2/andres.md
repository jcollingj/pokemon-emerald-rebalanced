# Andres (Ruin Maniac) — Route 105

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Larvitar | 16 | Smooth Rock | Sand Stream | Stealth Rock, Rock Slide, Crunch, Protect |
| Geodude | 18 | Wide Lens | Rock Head | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw, Protect |
| Nosepass | 18 | Leftovers | Sand Force | Power Gem, Thunder Wave, Rock Tomb, Rest |

## Puzzle
Andres is the entry hazard trainer. Larvitar leads with Stealth Rock + Sand Stream on turn 1 — Smooth Rock extends sandstorm to 8 turns. Every subsequent player switch-in takes Stealth Rock chip + sandstorm chip. Geodude builds Rollout via Defense Curl (Wide Lens keeps Rollout's accuracy reliable), threatening escalating damage that caps at 480 BP after five uses. Nosepass has Sand Force boosting Power Gem (Rock STAB ×1.3 in sand), Thunder Wave to paralyze, Rock Tomb for speed control, and Rest for sustain.

**The solve:** Water moves are super-effective against all three — but switching Water types in repeatedly means eating Stealth Rock + sandstorm chip every entry. The correct play is commit: bring in a Water type once and sweep through without switching. Alternatively, a Grass type handles all three without taking super-effective Rock hits. Against Geodude: don't let it get Defense Curl + Rollout going (Protect buys it a free turn). Prioritize Geodude before it snowballs.

## Design Notes
- Andres provides a completely different challenge from Foster: instead of setup sweepers and immovable walls, it's weather chip + hazards + escalating physical threat
- Larvitar at L16 is the same species the player faced on Roxanne's team — the player has seen Sand Stream before and should recognize the Stealth Rock turn 1 setup
- Wide Lens on Geodude brings Rollout from 90% to ~99% accuracy — the Defense Curl + Rollout combo is genuinely threatening if Geodude gets two free turns
- Sand Force on Nosepass makes Power Gem unexpectedly strong in sandstorm: effective 1.5× STAB × 1.3 Sand Force = a Rock nuke from what looks like a support Pokemon
- The "stay in and fight" lesson from Andres contrasts with Josue's "don't use status" lesson — Route 105 trains the player in distinct strategic habits before Brawly's gym
