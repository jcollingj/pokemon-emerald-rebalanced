# Ukyla (Swimmer F) — Route 106

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Tentacool | 16 | None | Poison Heal | Whirlpool, Acid Spray, Water Gun, Poison Sting |
| Wingull | 17 | Oran Berry | Keen Eye | Wing Attack, Water Pulse, Supersonic |
| Staryu | 18 | Eviolite | Illuminate | Psybeam, Power Gem, Water Gun, Recover |

## Puzzle
Kyla's team is built around setting up Staryu's Illuminate beams for maximum damage. Tentacool opens with Whirlpool (traps) + Acid Spray (guaranteed -2 SpDef), which makes the incoming Psybeam and Power Gem hit significantly harder. Wingull cleans up anything confused or weakened. The real threat is Eviolite Staryu: it soaks hits with boosted bulk, fires Psybeam (65 BP → 84.5 effective with Illuminate) or Power Gem (80 BP → 104 effective with Illuminate) for two different super-effective types, then Recovers when low. The player must prioritize KOing Tentacool quickly to protect SpDef, or use a physical attacker that isn't weakened by Acid Spray — but physical types still have to face Staryu's coverage beams.

## Design Notes
- Wingull here uses Keen Eye (accuracy can't drop) rather than Drizzle, differentiating it from Douglas's rain setup puzzle — same Pokemon, different role
- The Tentacool → Staryu sequence is a 2-stage trap: Acid Spray melts SpDef, then Illuminate beams punish the softened target
- Staryu at L18 with Eviolite has effective bulk comparable to a fully evolved L18 Pokemon — don't expect to OHKO it
- Power Gem and Psybeam hit different types: Power Gem covers Ice, Fire, Flying, Bug; Psybeam covers Ghost, Poison, Fighting — very few Pokemon resist both simultaneously
- Recover means the player must KO Staryu in 2 hits or it resets — requires either a strong OHKO threat or a physical attacker that doesn't care about the SpDef debuff
- This trainer previews Starmie's end-game role as a multi-beam coverage specialist
