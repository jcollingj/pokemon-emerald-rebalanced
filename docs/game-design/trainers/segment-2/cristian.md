# Cristian (Black Belt) — Dewford Gym

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Meditite | 18 | Eviolite | Pure Power | Fake Out, Drain Punch, Confusion, Detect |
| Machop | 19 | None | No Guard | Karate Chop, Seismic Toss, Focus Energy, Low Kick |
| Sableye | 19 | Sitrus Berry | Prankster | Will-O-Wisp, Feint Attack, Foul Play, Taunt |

## Puzzle

Cristian is the gym's final exam before Brawly — a condensed preview of three distinct mechanics the player will face again in the boss fight. Meditite opens: Pure Power makes Drain Punch hit for nearly twice what the stat line suggests, and healing scales with the boosted damage. Eviolite + Detect makes it hard to KO cleanly. Machop follows: No Guard means every move is 100% accurate. Focus Energy + Karate Chop crits constantly. Seismic Toss scales to Machop's level for reliable chip damage. Players who got comfortable with the earlier trainers get a rude awakening from "accurate everything + high crit." Sableye closes with Prankster Foul Play — uses the opponent's own Attack stat as the damage source, so bringing a strong physical sweeper to finish Sableye off actually backfires. Taunt denies any last-resort status moves.

## Design Notes
- Meditite's Detect in slot 4 is the critical detail — it predicts common "just spam the strongest move" finishers and forces prediction
- Machop's Focus Energy + No Guard + Karate Chop is the clearest pre-Brawly preview: it's Brawly's Machop but without Dynamic Punch yet
- Sableye's Foul Play is the closer's ace — players who send in their strongest physical attacker to clean up will find Foul Play dealing massive damage back using their own Atk stat
- No held item on Machop is intentional — it's the most aggressive Pokemon on the team, not the most durable; it gets in and deals damage
- Eviolite on Meditite + Sitrus Berry on Sableye means both of the "problematic" Pokemon have survivability tools; Machop is the middle aggressor
- Cristian winning requires the player to answer three different threats in sequence — the team doesn't have a single "break this one thing and sweep" solution
