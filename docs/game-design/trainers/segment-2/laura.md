# Laura (Laura) — Dewford Gym

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Makuhita | 17 | None | Iron Fist | Mach Punch, Fire Punch, Bulk Up, Tackle |
| Sableye | 18 | Sitrus Berry | Prankster | Will-O-Wisp, Feint Attack, Shadow Sneak, Taunt |
| Meditite | 18 | Eviolite | Pure Power | Fake Out, Drain Punch, Confusion, Detect |

## Puzzle

Laura is the first Sableye encounter in the gym — the fight that teaches the player "not everything here is weak to Psychic and Flying." Makuhita opens as a Fighting-type decoy, encouraging the player to bring their standard anti-Fighting answer (a Psychic or Flying type). Then Sableye comes in: Ghost/Dark typing with zero weaknesses. Prankster Will-O-Wisp burns the incoming Noctowl or Kirlia before they can act. Feint Attack is never-miss Dark STAB. Shadow Sneak is priority Ghost chip. Taunt shuts down any attempt to set up Calm Mind or Dragon Dance. If the player burns through Sableye, Meditite closes with Pure Power Drain Punch — hard to KO due to Eviolite, healing as it fights.

## Design Notes
- Sableye in slot 2 is the pivot. Lead Makuhita telegraphs "fighting gym = bring Psychic/Flying" and then punishes that instinct
- Prankster Will-O-Wisp fires before the player's Noctowl/Pidgeot/Kirlia can attack — the burn happens even if the player's Pokemon is faster
- Feint Attack can't miss — no Sand Attack or evasion cheating works against Sableye
- Taunt shuts down players who try to use status moves (Thunder Wave, Sleep Powder) to control Sableye
- Meditite's Detect in slot 4 gives it a predictable "protect" option — the player has to guess between Drain Punch and Detect
- Sitrus Berry on Sableye keeps it alive through one switch-in burst attack, giving Prankster burns time to stack
