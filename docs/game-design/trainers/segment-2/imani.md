# Imani (Swimmer F) — Route 105

## Team
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Tentacool | 17 | Toxic Orb | Poison Heal | Whirlpool, Scald, Acid Spray, Poison Sting |
| Wailmer | 18 | Leftovers | Blubber | Aqua Ring, Dive, Water Pulse, Water Gun |
| Wingull | 16 | None | Drizzle | Roost, Water Pulse, Wing Attack, Growl |

## Puzzle
Imani's team is defined by healing — everything refuses to die. Tentacool equips Toxic Orb to activate Poison Heal on turn 1: it heals 1/8 HP per turn, becomes immune to burn/paralysis/sleep, and walls special attacks with 100 SpDef. Wailmer stacks Blubber (1/8 per turn) + Leftovers (1/16 per turn) + Aqua Ring (1/16 per turn) for near-unkillable sustain in a Dive loop. Wingull has Roost for direct recovery and Drizzle to boost the whole team's Water moves.

**The solve:** Go fast and hit hard. These Pokemon don't threaten big damage — they outlast. High-power Electric or Grass moves to OHKO each before healing activates. Critical info: Tentacool with Toxic Orb is completely status-immune — burn, paralysis, and sleep are all wasted on it. Focus on raw damage output; status strategies backfire or do nothing here.

## Design Notes
- Imani is the defensive counterpart to Luis (offensive pressure) — her team demands that the player understand how each healing ability works
- Toxic Orb on Tentacool is the key "aha" moment: players who try to burn or paralyze Tentacool discover it's fully status-immune via Poison Heal
- Wailmer's triple healing (Blubber + Leftovers + Aqua Ring) is slow to fully activate: Aqua Ring must be set up first, and Dive buys time for the loop. A player who hits hard before Aqua Ring is set beats it cleanly.
- Wingull at L16 has minimal offensive power — its Roost makes it annoying to delete but not dangerous
- This team intentionally has low offensive output: the player faces a pure "do I have enough damage?" test, which contrasts with all other Route 105 trainers who have offensive threats
