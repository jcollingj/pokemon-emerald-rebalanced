# Segment 1 Trainers: Start to Rustboro Gym

## Design Rules
- All trainers have **3-6 Pokemon**
- Pokemon should be from the **local area** (what the player can catch nearby)
- A few surprise picks are fine, but most should be representative
- Every trainer is a **mini-puzzle** — competitive movesets, not filler
- Trainers should use Pokemon at appropriate levels for their route

## Route 116

### Clark (Hiker) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Accuracy Gauntlet" — Nincada drops accuracy, then Geodude's Rollout snowball punishes the player for staying in.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Nincada | 8 | — | Compound Eyes | Sand Attack, Scratch, Leech Life, Harden |
| Geodude | 9 | Wide Lens | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw, Tackle |
| Geodude | 10 | — | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw, Bulldoze |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Nincada opens with repeated Sand Attack to lower the player's accuracy. Clark then sends in Geodude, which Defense Curls and starts Rollout. Stamina means every hit Geodude takes boosts its Defense — attacking it only makes it tankier. Wide Lens on the lead Geodude gives 99% Rollout accuracy even through accuracy drops. The second Geodude has Bulldoze as a Ground STAB fallback if Rollout gets disrupted.
**Counter:** Use a Water or Grass special attacker before Sand Attack stacks pile up. Hitting Nincada immediately is ideal — without accuracy drops, Geodude is manageable. With accuracy drops, missing a Rollout sweep could be fatal.

### Devan (Hiker) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Punishment Machine" — Stealth Rock taxes switches, then the snowball closes.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Geodude | 9 | — | Sturdy | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw, Stealth Rock |
| Geodude | 10 | Wide Lens | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw, Bulldoze |
| Makuhita | 10 | — | Guts | Arm Thrust, Sand Attack, Focus Energy, Vital Throw |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Sturdy guarantees the lead Geodude survives one hit to set up Stealth Rock (every switch now costs HP) and get a Defense Curl off. The second Geodude is the snowball finisher with Stamina + Wide Lens. Makuhita is the wildcard: Focus Energy threatens critical hits, Sand Attack drops accuracy, and Vital Throw never misses regardless of evasion. Player can't freely switch Water/Grass types without taking SR chip first.
**Counter:** OHKO lead Geodude with a 2-hit sequence or spread damage before it sets SR. After SR is up, commit to one sweeper rather than switching. Watch out for Makuhita — Fighting type resists nothing the player commonly has at this point.
**Design Note:** Makuhita previews the Rusturf Tunnel encounter pool — the player can catch one right after this trainer.

### Mike (Hiker) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Wild Card" — expects a pure Rock team but hides a Flame Orb ambush.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Geodude | 9 | — | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw, Tackle |
| Whismur | 9 | — | Soundproof | Echoed Voice, Growl, Astonish, Stomp |
| Poochyena | 10 | Flame Orb | Guts | Bite, Quick Attack, Howl, Take Down |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Geodude leads as expected — player knows to use Water/Grass by now. Whismur introduces Soundproof (blocks Supersonic and other sound moves used against it) and Echoed Voice builds in power each consecutive turn. The real trap is Poochyena: Flame Orb activates Guts on turn 1, boosting all its moves by 1.5× immediately. Howl before Take Down/Bite creates a Level 10 attacker hitting way above its weight class.
**Counter:** KO Poochyena before it uses Howl — don't let it stack boosts. Whismur's Soundproof doesn't protect it from non-sound moves, so use standard STAB attacks. Water/Grass still handles Geodude.

### Jose (Bug Catcher) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Cutter Chain" — accuracy drops set up an unstoppable Fury Cutter chain.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Wurmple | 8 | — | Shield Dust | Poison Sting, String Shot, Bug Bite, Tackle |
| Nincada | 9 | — | Compound Eyes | Sand Attack, Scratch, Leech Life, Harden |
| Nincada | 10 | Oran Berry | Compound Eyes | Sand Attack, Fury Cutter, Leech Life, Metal Claw |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Wurmple opens with String Shot (speed drop) and Poison Sting (20% poison chance). First Nincada stacks Sand Attack to drop the player's accuracy. Second Nincada then uses Fury Cutter: starts at 40 BP but doubles with each consecutive hit (40→80→160→max), and Compound Eyes boosts accuracy to 132% — it nearly never misses. Stacked accuracy drops + Fury Cutter snowball = a chain the player can't afford to let start.
**Counter:** Immediately take out the second Nincada with a Flying, Fire, Rock, or Ground move before the Fury Cutter chain begins. Once it reaches 160 BP+ it's very hard to stop. Don't let Wurmple poison early or speed drops will compound the problem.

### Johnson (Youngster) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Status Net" — Stun Spore paralyzes, then Taillow and Whismur capitalize.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Shroomish | 8 | — | Technician | Tackle, Absorb, Bullet Seed, Stun Spore |
| Taillow | 9 | — | Guts | Pluck, Wing Attack, Quick Attack, Growl |
| Whismur | 9 | — | Soundproof | Echoed Voice, Astonish, Growl, Stomp |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Shroomish hits hard early with Bullet Seed (Technician turns it into a 2-5 hit move averaging ~75 effective BP) and threatens Stun Spore paralysis. Paralyzed Pokemon are 25% chance to not move + halved Speed. Taillow comes in as a surprisingly bulky wall (70/70/70 defenses from its rebalance) with Guts — if it somehow gets statused, it hits harder. Quick Attack lets it pick off weakened threats. Whismur's Soundproof blocks sound-based disruption moves, and Echoed Voice snowballs in power.
**Counter:** Rock or Electric types beat Taillow. Avoid spreading status (it buffs Taillow's Guts). Beat Shroomish fast before Stun Spore changes the pace of the battle.

### Darian (Fisherman) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Mirror Test" — teaches players that Water moves backfire on this specific team.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Magikarp | 8 | — | Swift Swim | Splash, Tackle |
| Tentacool | 9 | — | Liquid Ooze | Poison Sting, Wrap, Water Gun, Supersonic |
| Poliwag | 10 | — | Water Absorb | Water Gun, Hypnosis, Bubble, Rain Dance |
| Wingull | 10 | Sitrus Berry | Drizzle | Water Gun, Wing Attack, Growl, Water Pulse |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Magikarp is a decoy opener that lulls the player. Then the traps appear: Tentacool's Liquid Ooze means any HP-draining move (Leech Life, Mega Drain, Giga Drain) HURTS the user instead of healing. Poliwag's Water Absorb means Water-type moves HEAL it instead of dealing damage. Wingull sets Drizzle on switch-in, boosting all Water moves — but this helps Tentacool and Poliwag more than hurts the player. The lesson: don't spam Water moves thinking you're playing into the team's weakness.
**Counter:** Grass moves hit Tentacool and Poliwag. Electric or Rock STAB handles Wingull. Don't use drain moves against Tentacool, and absolutely don't throw Water moves at Poliwag.
**Design Note:** Darian placed right before Roxanne as a final reminder that Water coverage (which beats Roxanne) needs to be used intelligently. Some Water types are immune to their own type.

### Sarah (Lady) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Luxury Collection" — looks easy, hides a sleep + Adaptability burst combo.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Lotad | 8 | — | Rain Dish | Growl, Absorb, Astonish, Leech Seed |
| Skitty | 9 | — | Cute Charm | Tail Whip, Sing, Tackle, Attract |
| Eevee | 10 | Oran Berry | Adaptability | Quick Attack, Tackle, Sand Attack, Tail Whip |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Sarah appears to be a pushover "cute" trainer. Lotad opens by setting Leech Seed (passive drain per turn) and using Astonish for a 30% flinch chance. Skitty's Sing threatens sleep and Attract punishes opposite-gender Pokemon with 50% infatuation chance. The surprise is Eevee as the closer: Adaptability changes STAB from 1.5× to 2.0×, making Quick Attack (40 BP) hit at an effective 80 BP with +1 priority. A player who's been careless about HP drain or paralysis may suddenly face a fast, hard-hitting Normal closer.
**Counter:** Use a Grass-type immune to Leech Seed. Avoid contact moves against Skitty (Cute Charm 30% infatuation). KO Eevee before it Quick Attacks through a weakened team — it's frail but its opener can sweep if the player is at half health.

### Dawson (Rich Boy) — Optional
**Battle Theme:** "The Rich Kid Flex" — shows off rare Pokemon, but doesn't understand his own build.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Zigzagoon | 8 | Nugget | Pickup | Quick Attack, Covet, Headbutt, Belly Drum |
| Poochyena | 9 | — | Guts | Bite, Quick Attack, Howl, Take Down |
| Abra | 10 | — | Synchronize | Confusion, Psych Up, Teleport |

**Status:** designed
**Puzzle:** Dawson's Zigzagoon has a Nugget — but Pickup's enhanced Multiscale effect only works when itemless. His Zigzagoon is unprotected when it tries to Belly Drum, making the setup far riskier than it should be. The rich kid didn't read the guide. Poochyena with Guts is the real threat if it gets statused — and Dawson might accidentally status his own Poochyena by triggering Abra's Synchronize (reflects status conditions back at the attacker). Abra rounds things out with Confusion's special damage and Psych Up to copy stat boosts.
**Counter:** Hit Zigzagoon immediately before it Belly Drums — it's fully vulnerable since the Nugget disables Pickup. Avoid using status moves against Abra (Synchronize bounces them back). Poochyena is straightforward without a Flame Orb — its Guts won't activate unless the player accidentally poisons or burns it.
**Design Note:** Dawson teaches item-ability synergy via negative example. The player learns Pickup requires no held item, which reinforces the design for their own Zigzagoon. The Nugget drop after winning is the standard Rich Boy reward.
