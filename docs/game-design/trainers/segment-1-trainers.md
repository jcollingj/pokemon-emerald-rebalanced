- A few surprise picks are fine, but most should be representative
- Every trainer is a **mini-puzzle** — competitive movesets, not filler
- Trainers should use Pokemon at appropriate levels for their route

|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|

#### Scenario C: Player chose Torchic → Rival has Mudkip

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Mudkip | 7 | None | Rain Dish | Tackle, Growl, Mud-Slap |
| Zigzagoon | 6 | None | Pickup | Tackle, Growl, Sand Attack |
| Wingull | 5 | None | Drizzle | Water Gun, Growl |

**Status:** designed

**Design notes:**
- **Treecko** leads the Unburden identity immediately — physical hitter with high-crit Razor Leaf even before the item-consumption combo unlocks
- **Torchic** shows off the Speed Boost engine early — Bulk Up on turn 1 into Ember is real pressure, and the player sees Speed Boost ticking every turn even at this level
- **Mudkip** brings Mud-Slap (accuracy drop) + Rain Dish — sets up the defensive layering theme even without Rain Dance yet; if Wingull's Drizzle is active, Rain Dish heals Mudkip passively for extra tension
- **Zigzagoon** carries no item to activate Pickup's Multiscale-at-full-HP: the first hit does half damage, which surprises the player and teaches the Belly Drum build identity early. Cannot use Belly Drum yet (L14) but the behavior is already visible.
- **Wingull** is the surprise — found on Route 103 water, Drizzle activates on switch-in, immediately boosting Water Gun to 60 effective power. Teaches weather mechanics at the very start of the game. In Scenario C, Wingull's rain also activates Mudkip's Rain Dish, creating emergent synergy the player can observe.
- No items on any Pokemon — keeps the fight readable for new players while still showcasing each Pokemon's build identity
- Level curve (7 / 6 / 5) ensures the fight is a genuine challenge without being insurmountable with a solo starter

## Petalburg Woods

### Team Aqua Grunt — MANDATORY
| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Carvanha | 9 | | Strong Jaw | Bite, Leer, Aqua Jet |
| Tentacool | 10 | | Poison Heal | Poison Sting, Water Gun, Whirlpool |
| Corphish | 10 | | Sheer Force | Vise Grip, Leer, Bite |
| Poochyena | 11 | | Guts | Quick Attack, Bite, Scary Face |

**Status:** designed

**Design Notes:**
- **Carvanha (lead):** Fast opener at 75 Spe. Opens with Leer to chip Defense, then threatens with Strong Jaw-boosted Bite (STAB Dark, 180 effective BP off a L9 Pokemon) or Aqua Jet for priority. Teaches the player that Team Aqua Pokemon are speed-oriented and hit hard.
- **Tentacool (middle):** The annoying pivot. Whirlpool traps the player's Pokemon (buffed: traps + Atk -1), then stacks Poison Sting chip. 100 base SpDef means it tanks special hits. Forces the player to burn turns while their Pokemon can't escape — a skill-check that teaches what Tentacool does.
- **Corphish (middle):** Raw bruiser. Sheer Force-boosted Bite (flinch effect converted to 78 BP, plus STAB Dark = 117 effective) hits hard at L10. Vise Grip is the backup if they're in a bad matchup. Reinforces the Water/Dark Team Aqua identity.
- **Poochyena (closer):** 85 HP bulk makes this the last mon standing feel surprisingly tanky. Quick Attack priority and Bite STAB force the player to close out efficiently. Scary Face on the switch-in slows the player's mon, giving it at least one extra Bite turn. Guts synergy is flavor — no item to activate it here, but the threat of status is thematic.

**Team Identity:** This team teaches three things: Water/Dark is fast and powerful (Carvanha), Aqua types can lock you down (Tentacool trapping), and Dark STAB hits hard across multiple Pokemon (Carvanha + Corphish + Poochyena). The player should feel like they survived a real fight, not a pushover.

## Route 116

### Clark (Hiker) — Optional

**Team Identity:** "The Rollout Gauntlet" — Three Geodude that each restart the Stamina/Rollout snowball. Physical attackers can't break through; the player learns they need special moves or burst damage before the chain ramps.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Geodude | 8 | — | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Tackle |
| Geodude | 9 | — | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw |
| Geodude | 10 | — | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Rock Throw |

**Status:** designed

**Design Notes:**
- **Geodude #1 (L8):** Opens with Defense Curl (+1 Def, +1 Speed, doubles Rollout power), then starts the chain. 100 base Def + Stamina on every counter-hit means physical attackers can't out-damage it in time. Tackle as emergency fallback.
- **Geodude #2 (L9):** Same plan, adds Rock Throw as a reliable 65 BP Rock STAB if Rollout misses. Each new Geodude resets the chain — the player can't just outlast one.
- **Geodude #3 (L10):** The closer. Rock Throw gives reliable STAB when Rollout ends. By the third Geodude, cumulative Stamina + Defense Curl experience has taught the player exactly what's happening and forced them to find the right answer.
- **The puzzle:** Physical moves feed Stamina stacks. The player needs Water/Grass special coverage, high burst before the roll ramps, or a never-miss move (Swift, Aerial Ace). A concrete lesson that type coverage matters more than raw Attack.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|

| Geodude | 10 | — | Stamina | Defense Curl, Rollout, Bulldoze |
| Makuhita | 10 | — | Iron Fist | Fake Out, Mach Punch, Fire Punch |

**Status:** designed

**Design Notes:**
- **Geodude #1 (L9):** Hazard setter. Opens with Stealth Rock to punish switches, then Rock Throw for 65 BP STAB chip. Defense Curl is the backup if the player stalls. Teaches Stealth Rock as an early mechanic.
- **Geodude #2 (L10):** The full setup. Defense Curl into Rollout is the snowball threat; Bulldoze covers Ground STAB and drops Speed on whatever survives. Stamina stacks as before — physical attackers make it tankier.
- **Makuhita (L10):** The surprise. Fake Out flinches on the first turn (free turn). Mach Punch is +1 priority Iron Fist STAB — 48 effective BP, always goes first. Fire Punch (90 effective BP with Iron Fist) burns Grass types who switched in specifically to answer Geodude.
- **The puzzle:** Water/Grass counters the Geodudes but runs into Makuhita's Fire Punch. Fighting counters Makuhita but runs into Geodude's Rock Throw. The team punishes one-dimensional coverage — requires a two-type answer.

### Mike (Hiker) — Optional

**Team Identity:** "Accuracy Erosion" — Nincada duo drops accuracy to the floor while building Speed Boost stacks; Poochyena's Guts punishes any status the player tries in retaliation.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Nincada | 9 | — | Speed Boost | Sand Attack, Mud Slap, Scratch |
| Nincada | 9 | — | Compound Eyes | Fury Swipes, Sand Attack, Scratch |
| Poochyena | 11 | — | Guts | Howl, Bite, Quick Attack, Take Down |

**Status:** designed

**Design Notes:**
- **Nincada #1 (L9, Speed Boost):** The ramp. Sand Attack drops opponent accuracy; Mud Slap adds 20 BP Ground chip plus a second accuracy drop. Each turn it moves faster. After 2-3 turns at 90 base Def it's still alive and the opponent is at -2 accuracy.
- **Nincada #2 (L9, Compound Eyes):** The asymmetric harasser. Compound Eyes boosts Fury Swipes to ~94% accuracy per hit — the opponent is at -1 or -2 accuracy while Nincada is landing guaranteed multi-hit Normal STAB. Sand Attack continues stacking the drop.
- **Poochyena (L11):** The Guts closer. Howl for +1 Attack setup, then Bite (Dark STAB) + Quick Attack (priority) + Take Down (Normal STAB). Key interaction: if the player burns/paralyzes/poisons Poochyena to stop Howl setup, Guts activates (+1.5× Attack) — making the status attempt a trap.
- **The puzzle:** Accuracy drops make revenge-killing unreliable. Guts means status backfires. The answer: burst-KO Nincada fast before accuracy stacks, then hit Poochyena with a super effective move before Howl + Guts can stack.

### Johnson (Youngster) — Optional

**Team Identity:** "The Status Trap" — A youngster who doesn't know why everyone says status is overpowered. His whole team has Guts or Soundproof — status strategies completely backfire.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Whismur | 8 | — | Soundproof | Pound, Uproar |
| Poochyena | 9 | — | Guts | Quick Attack, Bite, Howl |
| Taillow | 10 | — | Guts | Wing Attack, Pluck, Tackle |

**Status:** designed

**Design Notes:**
- **Whismur (L8):** The opener. Soundproof makes it immune to sound-based moves (Supersonic, Screech). Uproar is a 90 BP Normal STAB that also prevents sleep for 3 turns — sleep strategies fail during Uproar. Pound for direct damage. Deceptively good lead that rejects two common early-game tactics at once. *(Note: Whismur's full rebalance design is not yet finalized — using conservative vanilla-style moves.)*
- **Poochyena (L9):** The Guts punisher. Howl for +1 Attack, then Bite (Dark STAB) + Quick Attack (Normal priority). If the player burns/paralyzes/poisons it trying to slow Howl setup, Guts activates: Howl + Guts + 85 HP = a very hard-hitting Pokemon they just made stronger.
- **Taillow (L10):** The Flying Guts finisher. Wing Attack (Flying STAB off 70/70/70 bulk) + Pluck (60 BP, item removal strips defensive Berries) + Tackle. Same Guts trap: status makes it hit harder, not slower.
- **The puzzle:** Burn, Toxic, Paralysis all activate Guts. Sleep fails thanks to Uproar. Screech/Supersonic fail to Soundproof. The intended answer is direct super-effective damage (Electric/Rock for Taillow, Fighting for Poochyena) without any status detours. Teaches the lesson that "always status first" is a bad habit.

**Status:** designed

**Team Identity:** "Rare Collection / Baton Pass Combo" — A rich kid showing off rare Route 116 Pokemon. Eevee sets up Agility and Baton Passes to Abra, which uses the Speed boost + Calm Mind for a nuclear sweep.

| Pokemon | Level | Item | Ability | Moves |
|---------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| Zigzagoon | 8 | Nugget | Pickup | Tackle, Growl, Sand Attack |
| Eevee | 9 | — | Adaptability | Swift, Agility, Bulk Up, Baton Pass |

**Status:** designed

**Design Notes:**
- **Zigzagoon (L8):** The money lead — holds a Nugget as Rich Boy flavor. Sand Attack for early accuracy drop, Growl for Atk chip, Tackle for pressure. *(Note: Nugget means Pickup's Multiscale-at-full-HP is inactive — Dawson's Zigzagoon prioritized the accessory over the defense.)*

Bulk Up (+1 Atk/Def) is an alternate pass option. Adaptability Swift (120 effective BP, never misses) is the fallback if Baton Pass is too risky.
- **Abra (L11):** The receiver and closer. Magic Guard blocks all passive damage — entry hazards, status chip, weather. Calm Mind: +1 SpA/SpD. Confusion: 50 BP Psychic STAB off 105 base SpA. Demonstrates that set-up + pass chains are one of the game's advanced strategies.
